\section{Executive Summary}
This project builds a supervised model to identify subscribers at high risk of cancelling
within the next 30 days, enabling proactive retention. Using twelve months of behavioral and
billing data for 84,000 subscribers, a gradient-boosted model achieves an AUC--ROC of 0.891
and, in a six-week pilot, reduced monthly churn in the treated cohort from 9.8\% to 7.1\%.

\section{Data \& Features}
The dataset joins subscription records, usage logs, and support interactions. After cleaning
and de-duplication, 84,012 active subscribers remained, with a 30-day forward churn label.

\section{Methodology}
We compared logistic regression, random forest, and gradient boosting under 5-fold
time-aware cross-validation, tuning with Bayesian optimization. The decision threshold was
chosen to maximize expected retention value, not raw accuracy:
\begin{equation}
\text{EV}(\tau)=\sum_{i:\,\hat p_i\ge\tau}\bigl(p_i\cdot v_{\text{save}}-c_{\text{offer}}\bigr),
\end{equation}
where $v_{\text{save}}$ is the retained lifetime value and $c_{\text{offer}}$ the incentive cost.

\section{Results}
Gradient boosting was selected for deployment. Beyond ranking accuracy, it produced
well-calibrated probabilities suitable for value-based thresholding.

\section{Deployment \& Recommendations}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=3pt]
  \item \textbf{Shadow (weeks 1--2):} score the base weekly; validate live vs.\ holdout.
  \item \textbf{Pilot (weeks 3--6):} trigger retention offers for 20\% of the at-risk segment,
        A/B tested against a control to confirm causal lift.
  \item \textbf{Rollout (weeks 7--12):} automate weekly batch scoring; add drift monitoring
        (PSI, calibration, performance-decay alerts).
\end{enumerate}

\section{Limitations}
The label horizon is fixed at 30 days; longer horizons need re-labeling. The pilot ran on a
single market, and seasonal effects were not fully controlled. Ongoing monitoring for
feature drift is required to sustain performance.
