%% ── Section 2: Purpose and Background ────────────────────────────────────────
\begin{tcolorbox}[jspsSection,
  title={\faFile\enspace \bilabel{Research Background and Objectives}{研究の背景と目的} \hfill \small Section 2}]

\textbf{\textcolor{jspsNavy}{Background \textcolor{jspsSlate}{\small（背景）}}}

The question of whether quantum mechanical phenomena play a functional role in biological neural systems remains one of the most provocative open problems in biophysics. Recent experimental work at Meridian Institute and elsewhere has demonstrated anomalous long-lived vibrational coherence in isolated tubulin dimers at physiological temperatures (310 K), with coherence lifetimes in the 50--200\,fs window --- far exceeding predictions from classical Redfield theory. Critically, cryo-electron tomography data from the Synapse Medical Research Center collaborating group (Tanaka et al., \textit{Nat.\ Neurosci.}, 2024) reveals that microtubule bundles within hippocampal pyramidal neurons adopt a quasi-crystalline lateral packing geometry that may act as a ``phonon waveguide'', suppressing environmental noise through collective mode engineering.

Despite this tantalizing evidence, two fundamental gaps remain. \textbf{First}, no theoretical framework exists that seamlessly bridges quantum transport at the tubulin dimer scale ($\sim$10\,nm) with mesoscale neural network dynamics ($\sim$10\,mm). \textbf{Second}, no direct in-vivo measurement has demonstrated a causal link between microtubule coherence and observable electrophysiological signatures such as gamma-band ($\gamma$, 30--100\,Hz) oscillations.

\vspace{4pt}
\textbf{\textcolor{jspsNavy}{Objectives \textcolor{jspsSlate}{\small（目的）}}}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em, label=\textbf{O\arabic*.}, itemsep=1pt]
  \item Derive and validate a multi-scale open-quantum-systems model linking tubulin dimer coherence to microtubule bundle phonon modes and ultimately to neural network firing statistics.
  \item Perform targeted pharmacological perturbation experiments using Taxol and nocodazole in hippocampal slice preparations to isolate microtubule-specific contributions to $\gamma$-band LFPs.
  \item Apply two-dimensional electronic spectroscopy (2DES) to fresh cortical tissue biopsies to obtain a direct spectroscopic signature of quantum coherence in intact neural tissue.
  \item Develop an open-source computational pipeline (\texttt{NeuroQuant}) integrating density-matrix simulation with spiking network models, to be released via the Nimbus Open Science Platform.
\end{enumerate}

\end{tcolorbox}

%% ── Section 3: Methodology ───────────────────────────────────────────────────
\begin{tcolorbox}[jspsSection,
  title={\faPen\enspace \bilabel{Research Methods}{研究方法} \hfill \small Section 3}]

\textbf{\textcolor{jspsNavy}{3.1\enspace Theoretical Framework}}

We adopt the Hierarchical Equations of Motion (HEOM) formalism to model the quantum dynamics of the tubulin--water bath interaction. The system Hamiltonian is partitioned as:
\[
  \hat{H}_{\mathrm{tot}} = \hat{H}_S + \hat{H}_B + \hat{H}_{SB}
\]
where $\hat{H}_S$ is the electronic Hamiltonian of the tubulin dimer lattice (obtained from quantum-chemistry TDDFT calculations at the PBE0/6-311+G* level), $\hat{H}_B$ denotes the protein-solvent phonon bath, and $\hat{H}_{SB}$ is the system--bath coupling modelled via a Drude--Lorentz spectral density:
\[
  J(\omega) = \frac{2\lambda \gamma \omega}{\omega^2 + \gamma^2}
\]
with reorganization energy $\lambda$ and bath correlation time $\gamma^{-1}$ fit to experimental 2DES data. The HEOM hierarchy is truncated at depth $\mathcal{N} = 6$ and solved numerically using a GPU-accelerated Julia solver developed within our group.

\vspace{4pt}
\textbf{\textcolor{jspsNavy}{3.2\enspace Experimental Components}}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em, itemsep=1pt]
  \item \textit{Hippocampal slice electrophysiology}: Acute 400\,$\mu$m coronal slices from Wistar rats maintained at 34$^\circ$C in ACSF. Multi-electrode arrays (MEA, 256-channel) record spontaneous and carbachol-evoked $\gamma$ oscillations. Microtubule integrity will be graded in parallel via immunofluorescence ($\alpha$-tubulin antibody, clone DM1A).
  \item \textit{2D Electronic Spectroscopy}: A 10\,fs, 20\,nJ broadband pump--probe apparatus (home-built, Meridian Optics Lab) will be deployed on fresh cortical punch biopsies from surgically resected tissue (ethics approval: Meridian IRB \#2025-0441). The rephasing and non-rephasing signal contributions will be separated via phase cycling.
  \item \textit{Cryo-ET and image analysis}: Collaboration with Tanaka lab at Synapse Medical Research Center; focused-ion-beam milling on vitrified hippocampal slices followed by sub-tomogram averaging to resolve microtubule lattice spacing to better than 2\,\AA{} precision.
\end{itemize}

\vspace{4pt}
\textbf{\textcolor{jspsNavy}{3.3\enspace Computational Pipeline (\texttt{NeuroQuant})}}

The \texttt{NeuroQuant} software will consist of three loosely coupled modules:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em, label=(\arabic*), itemsep=1pt]
  \item \textit{QuantumCore}: HEOM solver with automatic bath parameterization from spectroscopic input.
  \item \textit{MesoBridge}: Maps quantum transport rates to phenomenological conductance parameters in a multi-compartment neuron model (NEURON simulator backend).
  \item \textit{NetSim}: Leaky integrate-and-fire network simulator with realistic cortical topology seeded from Allen Brain Connectivity Atlas.
\end{enumerate}
All code will be published under the MIT License via Nimbus Open Science Platform (DOI reserved: 10.xxxxx/neurq.2026).

\end{tcolorbox}

  \begin{tcolorbox}[jspsField, title=Scientific Significance \small（学術的意義）]
    \small
    If quantum coherence is confirmed as functionally relevant in neural tissue, it would necessitate a fundamental revision of both computational neuroscience models and theories of neural synchrony. Our cross-scale framework represents the \textbf{first rigorous quantum-to-network bridge} in the literature, enabling quantitative predictions testable by MEA electrophysiology. This positions the project to resolve a 30-year-old debate initiated by Penrose and Hameroff, now with experimentally grounded methodology.
  \end{tcolorbox}
\end{minipage}%

Our GPU-accelerated HEOM solver achieves a 40$\times$ speedup over existing CPU implementations, enabling parameter sweeps previously infeasible. The \texttt{NeuroQuant} open-source pipeline will serve the international quantum biology community, reinforcing Japan's leadership in this emerging interdisciplinary field.
  \end{tcolorbox}
\end{minipage}
