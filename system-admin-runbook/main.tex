\section{System Architecture Overview}

The Nimbus Cloud Platform (NCP) is a multi-tenant cloud infrastructure platform serving enterprise customers across three geographic regions: us-east, eu-west, and ap-southeast. The platform processes approximately 2.8 million API requests per minute at peak load and maintains a 99.95\% uptime SLA for tier-1 customers.

\begin{infobox}
NCP runs on a containerized microservices architecture orchestrated by the NCP Orchestrator. All production services are deployed across a minimum of three availability zones per region. Database services use active-passive replication with automated failover triggered at 30 seconds of primary unresponsiveness.
\end{infobox}

\subsection*{Core Production Services}

\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{llX}
\toprule
\textbf{Service} & \textbf{Port} & \textbf{Description} \\
\midrule
ncp-api-gateway       & 8443 & Primary ingress controller; terminates TLS, enforces rate limits, routes requests to backend services. \\
ncp-auth-service      & 9090 & Identity and access management; OAuth 2.0 / OIDC provider with hardware MFA enforcement. \\
ncp-database-primary  & 5432 & Managed PostgreSQL 15 cluster; primary write node for all transactional and session data. \\
ncp-message-queue     & 5672 & Asynchronous message broker; handles event streaming, job dispatch, and dead-letter queues. \\
ncp-object-store      & 9000 & S3-compatible blob storage for customer assets, database backups, and static content delivery. \\
ncp-orchestrator      & 6443 & Container orchestration control plane; manages service deployment, autoscaling, and health probes. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}

\section{Alert Severity Classification}

Every incident must be assigned a severity level at the moment of declaration. The severity determines response time commitments, escalation paths, and post-incident review requirements.

\medskip
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{cll>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X}
\toprule
\textbf{Level} & \textbf{Label} & \textbf{Response} & \textbf{Definition \& Examples} \\
\midrule
\rowcolor{alertred!15} SEV-1 & Critical & $<5$ minutes & Complete service outage affecting multiple regions or all customers. Data loss or corruption in progress. Security breach with active exploitation. \textbf{Example:} ncp-api-gateway returning 503 across all zones. \\
\rowcolor{amber!15} SEV-2 & Major     & $<15$ minutes & Significant service degradation. Partial outage affecting a single region or a subset of customers. \textbf{Example:} ncp-auth-service latency above 5 seconds in eu-west. \\
\rowcolor{infoblue!10} SEV-3 & Minor     & $<1$ hour   & Isolated issue affecting a single customer or non-critical component. No data loss risk. \textbf{Example:} ncp-object-store slow listing for one tenant bucket. \\
SEV-4 & Informational & $<24$ hours & Cosmetic issue, planned maintenance notification, or documentation gap. No customer impact. \textbf{Example:} Monitoring dashboard showing stale CPU metrics for ncp-message-queue. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}

\vspace{4pt}
\begin{critbox}
\textbf{SEV-1 Declaration Authority:} Only the on-call Lead SRE or Infrastructure Manager may declare or downgrade a SEV-1 incident. Any engineer may declare SEV-2 through SEV-4. All severity changes must be logged in the incident channel with justification.
\end{critbox}

\section{Common Operational Procedures}

\subsection{Procedure 4.1: Service Restart}

Restarting a production service requires connection draining and health verification. Never restart more than one service simultaneously without explicit approval from the Infrastructure Manager.

\medskip
\begin{stepbox}
\textbf{Step 1 --- Assess Current State}\smallskip

Query the service status and note any active alerts or anomalies.
\end{stepbox}

\begin{cmdbox}
\begin{Verbatim}[fontsize=\footnotesize]
$ ncp-cli service status ncp-api-gateway
SERVICE              STATUS    UPTIME     CPU%   MEM%    CONNS
ncp-api-gateway      RUNNING   14d 3h      12%   2.1G     1,847
\end{Verbatim}
\end{cmdbox}

\begin{stepbox}
\textbf{Step 2 --- Notify Affected Teams}\smallskip

Post a heads-up in \texttt{\#ncp-incidents} with the service name, planned restart window, and expected impact duration. Wait for acknowledgement from at least one team lead before proceeding.
\end{stepbox}

\begin{stepbox}
\textbf{Step 3 --- Drain Connections}\smallskip

Enable drain mode to stop new connections from reaching the instance while allowing existing requests to complete.
\end{stepbox}

\begin{cmdbox}
\begin{Verbatim}[fontsize=\footnotesize]
$ ncp-cli drain enable ncp-api-gateway --timeout 90
Drain mode enabled. 1,847 active connections. Waiting...
All connections drained (87s). Safe to proceed.
\end{Verbatim}
\end{cmdbox}

\begin{stepbox}
\textbf{Step 4 --- Execute Restart}\smallskip

Perform a graceful restart and monitor the service log for errors.
\end{stepbox}

\begin{cmdbox}
\begin{Verbatim}[fontsize=\footnotesize]
$ ncp-cli restart ncp-api-gateway --graceful
Stopping ncp-api-gateway... [OK]
Starting ncp-api-gateway... [OK]
$ ncp-cli logs ncp-api-gateway --since 2m | grep -i error
(no output -- clean restart)
\end{Verbatim}
\end{cmdbox}

\begin{stepbox}
\textbf{Step 5 --- Verify Health}\smallskip

Confirm the service is accepting traffic and responding within SLA thresholds.
\end{stepbox}

\begin{cmdbox}
\begin{Verbatim}[fontsize=\footnotesize]
$ ncp-cli health-check ncp-api-gateway --sla
HEALTH CHECK: ncp-api-gateway
Status:       HEALTHY
Latency p99:  42ms (SLA: < 100ms)
Error rate:   0.00% (SLA: < 0.01%)
All checks passed.
\end{Verbatim}
\end{cmdbox}

\begin{successbox}
Service restart completed successfully. Update the incident ticket if one was open and post a confirmation in \texttt{\#ncp-incidents}. Disable drain mode if it was not automatically cleared: \texttt{\$ ncp-cli drain disable ncp-api-gateway}.
\end{successbox}

\subsection{Procedure 4.2: Disk Space Emergency Cleanup}

Low disk space on production nodes can cause service failures, database corruption, and log loss. Act immediately when any filesystem exceeds 85\% utilization.

\begin{warningbox}
\textbf{Do not} delete files from \texttt{/data/ncp/database/} or \texttt{/data/ncp/object-store/} manually. These paths are managed by their respective services and improper cleanup can cause irrecoverable data loss.
\end{warningbox}

\begin{stepbox}
\textbf{Step 1 --- Identify Consumption}\smallskip

Locate the largest directories and file types consuming space.
\end{stepbox}

\begin{cmdbox}
\begin{Verbatim}[fontsize=\footnotesize]
$ df -h /data/ncp
Filesystem      Size   Used   Avail   Use%   Mounted on
/dev/sdb1       500G   462G    14G    97%    /data/ncp

$ du -sh /data/ncp/* | sort -rh | head -5
187G    /data/ncp/logs
142G    /data/ncp/object-store
 89G    /data/ncp/database
 31G    /data/ncp/temp
 13G    /data/ncp/backups
\end{Verbatim}
\end{cmdbox}

\begin{stepbox}
\textbf{Step 2 --- Rotate and Compress Logs}\smallskip

Archive logs older than 7 days and remove raw logs older than 30 days.
\end{stepbox}

\begin{cmdbox}
\begin{Verbatim}[fontsize=\footnotesize]
$ ncp-logrotate --service all --older-than 7d --compress
Rotating logs older than 7 days...
  ncp-api-gateway:      compressed 2.3G -> 0.4G
  ncp-auth-service:     compressed 1.1G -> 0.2G
  ncp-database-primary: compressed 4.7G -> 0.9G
Done. Freed approximately 6.6G.

$ find /data/ncp/logs -name "*.log" -mtime +30 -delete
$ echo $?
0
\end{Verbatim}
\end{cmdbox}

\begin{stepbox}
\textbf{Step 3 --- Purge Temporary Files}\smallskip

Clear the temp directory. Confirm no active processes are writing to files being removed.
\end{stepbox}

\begin{cmdbox}
\begin{Verbatim}[fontsize=\footnotesize]
$ lsof +D /data/ncp/temp 2>/dev/null | wc -l
0
$ rm -rf /data/ncp/temp/*
$ df -h /data/ncp | grep /data/ncp
/dev/sdb1       500G   412G    64G    87%    /data/ncp
\end{Verbatim}
\end{cmdbox}

\begin{successbox}
Disk usage reduced from 97\% to 87\%. Monitor for 24 hours. If usage trends above 90\% within that window, escalate to the Infrastructure Manager for capacity planning.
\end{successbox}

\subsection{Procedure 4.3: TLS Certificate Renewal}

Production TLS certificates are managed by ncp-certbot with automated renewal. Manual intervention is required only when automated renewal fails or when certificates are within 72 hours of expiry.

\begin{warningbox}
A certificate expiry on ncp-api-gateway will cause a complete platform outage (SEV-1). Certificate renewal must never be deferred once expiry is within the 7-day warning window.
\end{warningbox}

\begin{stepbox}
\textbf{Step 1 --- Check Certificate Status}\smallskip

List all managed certificates and their remaining validity periods.
\end{stepbox}

\begin{cmdbox}
\begin{Verbatim}[fontsize=\footnotesize]
$ ncp-certbot list
CERTIFICATE                  EXPIRY       REMAINING   STATUS
api.nimbuscloud.io           2026-08-12   28 days     VALID
auth.nimbuscloud.io          2026-07-18    3 days     RENEWAL_DUE
console.nimbuscloud.io       2026-09-01   48 days     VALID
\end{Verbatim}
\end{cmdbox}

\begin{stepbox}
\textbf{Step 2 --- Execute Renewal}\smallskip

Renew certificates flagged as \texttt{RENEWAL\_DUE}. The \texttt{-{}-dry-run} flag validates without committing changes.
\end{stepbox}

\begin{cmdbox}
\begin{Verbatim}[fontsize=\footnotesize]
$ ncp-certbot renew auth.nimbuscloud.io --dry-run
Dry run: auth.nimbuscloud.io
Challenge type:   DNS-01
DNS propagation:  OK (4.3s)
Certificate issued by: Let's Encrypt R11
Dry run successful. Ready to renew.

$ ncp-certbot renew auth.nimbuscloud.io
Renewing auth.nimbuscloud.io...
New certificate fingerprint: A7:F3:...:9C
Valid until: 2026-10-16
\end{Verbatim}
\end{cmdbox}

\begin{stepbox}
\textbf{Step 3 --- Reload Affected Services}\smallskip

Services that terminated TLS using the old certificate must reload their configuration.
\end{stepbox}

\begin{cmdbox}
\begin{Verbatim}[fontsize=\footnotesize]
$ ncp-cli reload-certs ncp-auth-service
Reloading TLS certificates for ncp-auth-service... [OK]
New certificate active. Expires: 2026-10-16.
\end{Verbatim}
\end{cmdbox}

\begin{successbox}
\textbf{Post-Renewal Verification}\\[2pt]
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}l>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X@{}}
\texttt{ncp-certbot list} & Confirm all certificates show VALID status \\
\texttt{curl -svI ...} & Verify the new expiry date appears in the TLS handshake \\
\texttt{ncp-cli health-check ncp-auth-service} & Confirm service health after certificate reload \\
\end{tabularx}
\end{successbox}

\section{Incident Response Workflow}

When an incident is detected (alert, customer report, or engineer observation), follow this workflow without deviation.

\begin{infobox}
\textbf{Incident Channel:} All incident communication happens in \texttt{\#ncp-incidents}. Do not use direct messages or side channels for incident coordination. The incident channel is the single source of truth for post-incident review.
\end{infobox}

\medskip
\begin{stepbox}
\textbf{\faPlay\ \ Incident Response Checklist}\smallskip

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=4pt]
  \item \textbf{Declare:} Acknowledge the alert in \texttt{\#ncp-incidents}. Assign a severity level per Section~3. Start a timer.
  \item \textbf{Triage:} Identify the affected service, region, and customer scope. Post findings to the channel.
  \item \textbf{Mitigate:} Apply the relevant procedure from Section~4. If no procedure matches, escalate to the Lead SRE immediately.
  \item \textbf{Communicate:} Post a status update every 15 minutes for SEV-1, every 30 minutes for SEV-2. Include: current state, action in progress, ETA to resolution.
  \item \textbf{Resolve:} Confirm that all health checks pass and affected customers are restored. Post the resolution time and root cause summary.
  \item \textbf{Review:} Within 48 hours, the incident owner must draft a post-incident review document covering timeline, root cause, impact, and preventive actions.
\end{enumerate}
\end{stepbox}

\begin{critbox}
\textbf{SEV-1 Communication Template}\smallskip

Copy and paste into \texttt{\#ncp-incidents} at declaration and every 15-minute update:\smallskip

\texttt{[SEV-1] \{service-name\} --- \{one-line summary\}}\\
\texttt{Declared:} \{timestamp UTC\}\\
\texttt{Incident Lead:} \{name\}\\
\texttt{Affected:} \{regions / customer count / feature\}\\
\texttt{Current Action:} \{what is being done right now\}\\
\texttt{Next Update:} \{timestamp + 15 min\}
\end{critbox}
