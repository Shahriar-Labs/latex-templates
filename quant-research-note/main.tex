\section{Executive Summary}

We propose a systematic mean-reversion strategy in the S\&P 500 universe,
exploiting short-term price overreaction. The signal composites normalized
1-day and 5-day reversals with volatility-adjusted entry thresholds. Over a
3-year backtest (Jan 2021--Dec 2023), the strategy delivers a Sharpe of
\textbf{2.31} with zero market beta after hedging. Capacity estimates
suggest scalability to \$800M AUM before slippage exceeds 15\,bp per trade.

\section{Signal Construction}

The alpha signal is a rank-weighted composite of three sub-signals,
recalibrated monthly:

\vspace{2pt}
{\footnotesize
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}>{\color{navy}\bfseries}l@{\hspace{5mm}}>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X@{}}
$R_{1d}$   & Normalized 1-day return reversal ($t-1$ close to $t$ close),\\
           & cross-sectionally z-scored within GICS sector.\\
$R_{5d}$   & Normalized 5-day return reversal, winsorized at $\pm 3\sigma$.\\
$V_{adj}$  & Volatility adjustment factor: $1/\sigma_{20d}$, dampened below\\
           & the 10th percentile to avoid micro-cap noise.\\
\end{tabularx}
}

\vspace{4pt}
The composite score is $S = 0.45\,R_{1d} + 0.35\,R_{5d} + 0.20\,V_{adj}$.
We take long positions in the top decile and short the bottom decile,
rebalancing daily with a \$1 notional per side, market-neutral.

\section{Backtest Results}

The strategy exhibits consistent performance across regimes (Table~\ref{tab:perf}).
Notably, it performed strongly during the 2022 rate-hiking cycle, where
trend-following strategies struggled, confirming the diversifying nature
of the reversal premium.

\section{Risk \& Implementation}

\begin{alertbox}
\textbf{Key Risks:} (i)~Crowding in the reversal factor may compress
spreads if AUM in similar strategies grows beyond \$5B. (ii)~Tail events
during liquidity crises (Mar~2020-style) require a volatility-scaled
leverage overlay. (iii)~Short-selling costs in the lowest-decile names
average 35\,bp annually; included in net PnL above.
\end{alertbox}

\vspace{3pt}
Execution via a single-queue TWAP algorithm minimizes market impact.
Turnover averages 18\% per day (both legs), implying 46$\times$ annualized;
commissions and slippage total 22\,bp, already netted from reported returns.
We recommend a 200\,bp stop-loss at the portfolio level with a 3-day
cooling-off period after a trigger.
