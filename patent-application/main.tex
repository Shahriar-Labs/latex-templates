\section{Field of the Invention}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The present invention relates generally to thermal management of electrochemical energy
storage devices, and more particularly to microchannel-based heat-exchange systems
incorporating phase-change materials (PCMs) for regulating operating temperatures of
high-density solid-state battery modules used in electric vehicles and grid-storage
applications.

% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{Background of the Invention}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

High-density solid-state battery modules generate substantial heat during rapid charge and
discharge cycles. Conventional thermal-management approaches relying on liquid cooling
plates or air-cooled fins have proven inadequate as cell-level power densities exceed
\mbox{500\,W/kg}. Excessive thermal gradients across a module degrade ionic conductivity in
solid electrolyte layers, accelerate lithium dendrite nucleation at grain boundaries, and
reduce cycle life by as much as 40\% (see, e.g., Smith et al., \textit{J.~Power Sources},
vol.~511, 2024, pp.~1--18).

Prior art phase-change systems (U.S.~Pat.\ No.~10,847,223 to Chen; U.S.~Pat.\ No.
11,201,366 to Nakamura et al.) disclose encapsulated PCM slurries in macro-scale channels.
These designs suffer from non-uniform PCM distribution, high pumping parasitic losses, and
inability to dynamically redirect coolant flow in response to localised hot spots.

There remains a need for a thermally adaptive system that (a)~achieves uniform temperature
distribution within $\pm 1\,^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ across a 48-cell module, (b)~operates without
external pumping power during Phase~I of the thermal cycle, and (c)~supports in-situ PCM
re-solidification without module disassembly.

% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{Summary of the Invention}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

In one aspect, the invention provides an adaptive thermal-management assembly comprising: a
plurality of microchannel plates (\textbf{100}) interleaved between solid-state battery
cells (\textbf{102}); a network of microchannels (\textbf{104}) formed within each plate,
each microchannel having a hydraulic diameter in the range of 200--800\,\textmu m; a
phase-change material (\textbf{106}) disposed within a sealed reservoir (\textbf{108})
fluidly coupled to the microchannel network; and an adaptive valve array (\textbf{110})
operable by shape-memory alloy actuators (\textbf{112}) to selectively direct PCM flow
toward regions of elevated thermal load.

In another aspect, the invention provides a method of thermally managing a solid-state
battery module, comprising: sensing a temperature differential exceeding a threshold of
$2\,^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ between any two battery cells (\textbf{102}); actuating at least one
shape-memory alloy valve (\textbf{112}) to redirect molten PCM (\textbf{106}) from a cooler
microchannel region to the hotter region; and passively re-solidifying the PCM upon
cessation of the thermal load without external actuation.

These and other features of the invention are set forth more particularly in the Detailed
Description that follows.

\section{Detailed Description of the Preferred Embodiments}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Reference is now made to the drawings in which like reference numerals designate like or
corresponding parts throughout the several views.

\subsection*{I.\quad Microchannel Plate Assembly (100)}

As shown in FIG.~1 and FIG.~2, the microchannel plate (\textbf{100}) is fabricated from
thermally conductive aluminium alloy 6061-T6 having a bulk thermal conductivity of
167\,W/m\,K. The plate (\textbf{100}) is 1.5\,mm thick and is dimensioned to match the
footprint of each prismatic battery cell (\textbf{102}), nominally 148\,mm $\times$ 98\,mm.
A serpentine array of microchannels (\textbf{104}) is formed by photochemical etching to a
depth of 400\,\textmu m and a width of 300\,\textmu m, yielding a hydraulic diameter
$D_h = 343$\,\textmu m. The channel walls (\textbf{114}) are coated with a
30\,nm-thick layer of titanium nitride (TiN) by physical vapour deposition to prevent
corrosion from the PCM (\textbf{106}).

\subsection*{II.\quad Phase-Change Material (106) and Reservoir (108)}

The PCM (\textbf{106}) in the preferred embodiment is a binary paraffin eutectic
(n-eicosane/n-octadecane, 60:40 wt.\%) exhibiting a melting onset temperature of
$33.5\,^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ and a latent heat of 218\,kJ/kg. The sealed reservoir (\textbf{108}) is
housed within an anodised aluminium enclosure (\textbf{116}) mounted to one lateral edge of
the module, providing a PCM charge of 85\,g per 12-cell tier. A flexible graphite membrane
(\textbf{118}) at the reservoir outlet accommodates volumetric expansion of up to 12\% upon
PCM melting without mechanical stress on seals.

\subsection*{III.\quad Adaptive Valve Array (110) and SMA Actuators (112)}

The adaptive valve array (\textbf{110}), detailed in FIG.~3, comprises a set of
binary-position microvalves (\textbf{120}) disposed at each microchannel branch junction.
Each microvalve (\textbf{120}) is actuated by a shape-memory alloy (Nitinol, 50.8 at.\%~Ni)
wire actuator (\textbf{112}) of diameter 100\,\textmu m. The Nitinol actuator (\textbf{112})
has a transformation temperature of $35\,^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$; when the local cell surface temperature
exceeds this threshold, the actuator (\textbf{112}) contracts by 4\% of its 20\,mm free
length, rotating the valve disc (\textbf{122}) by $90^{\circ}$ to the open position and
admitting molten PCM (\textbf{106}) flow through the adjacent microchannel (\textbf{104}).

Temperature sensors (\textbf{124}) -- NTC thermistors with sensitivity of
$-4.4\,\%\,^{\circ}\mathrm{C}^{-1}$ -- are embedded in each cell-facing surface of the plate
(\textbf{100}) and connected to a battery management controller (\textbf{126}) via a
flexible printed circuit (\textbf{128}). The controller (\textbf{126}) executes a
proportional-integral control law with a 50\,ms sampling interval, commanding individual
SMA actuators (\textbf{112}) through pulse-width-modulated drive signals.

\subsection*{IV.\quad Performance and Re-Solidification}

During a 3C discharge cycle at an ambient temperature of $25\,^{\circ}$C, prototype testing
demonstrated that the assembly maintained cell temperatures within
$\pm 0.8\,^{\circ}$C across all 48 cells for 95\% of the discharge duration (FIG.~4),
compared with $\pm 4.3\,^{\circ}$C for a conventional aluminium cooling plate of equivalent
mass. Upon completion of the discharge cycle, the PCM (\textbf{106}) re-solidifies
passively within 12 minutes, driven by conduction to ambient through the module housing,
without any external pumping power or heater intervention.

\subsection*{V.\quad Alternative Embodiments}

In an alternative embodiment, the PCM (\textbf{106}) may be replaced by a microencapsulated
PCM slurry (\textbf{130}) dispersed in a dielectric carrier fluid (perfluorocarbon, 3M
Novec\texttrademark~7500), enabling active pumped-loop operation at power densities
exceeding 800\,W/kg while retaining the latent-heat buffering benefit. In yet another
embodiment, the microchannel plate (\textbf{100}) may be formed from a carbon-fibre-reinforced
polymer composite (\textbf{132}) for weight-critical aerospace applications.

The foregoing description of the preferred embodiments is presented for purposes of
illustration and description. It is not intended to be exhaustive or to limit the invention to
the precise forms disclosed. Modifications and variations are possible in light of the above
teachings.

  operatively connected to a plurality of temperature sensors embedded in respective

  $-3\,\%\,^{\circ}\mathrm{C}^{-1}$, and wherein the controller samples the temperature

\claim{The assembly of claim~1, wherein the sealed reservoir further comprises a
  flexible membrane configured to accommodate volumetric expansion of the phase-change
  material upon melting of at least 10\% without mechanical stress on seals of the

  sensing, by a plurality of temperature sensors, a temperature differential between at
  least two solid-state battery cells exceeding a predetermined threshold;\newline
  actuating, in response to the sensed temperature differential, at least one microvalve of
  an adaptive valve array to redirect flow of a phase-change material within a microchannel

  passively re-solidifying the phase-change material within the microchannel network upon

\section{Abstract}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

An adaptive thermal-management assembly for high-density solid-state battery modules
comprises aluminium microchannel plates (\textbf{100}) interleaved between prismatic
battery cells (\textbf{102}), a sealed reservoir (\textbf{108}) containing a binary paraffin
eutectic phase-change material (PCM) (\textbf{106}) with a melting point near
33.5\,\textdegree C, and an adaptive valve array (\textbf{110}) actuated by Nitinol
shape-memory alloy wires (\textbf{112}). Temperature sensors (\textbf{124}) embedded in each
plate report to a battery management controller (\textbf{126}), which executes a
proportional-integral control law to direct molten PCM selectively toward regions of
elevated thermal load. The assembly maintains temperature uniformity within
$\pm 1\,^{\circ}$C across a 48-cell module during 3C discharge cycles without external
pumping power. A flexible graphite membrane (\textbf{118}) accommodates PCM volumetric
expansion, and the PCM re-solidifies passively upon cooling. The invention extends
solid-state battery cycle life by reducing thermally induced electrolyte degradation and
dendrite formation.
