\section{Abstract}

This experiment investigates an inverting amplifier and a second-order
active low-pass filter built around the LM741 operational amplifier. The
closed-loop gain of the inverting stage was measured at five frequencies
between \qty{100}{\Hz} and \qty{100}{\kHz}, yielding a mid-band gain of
$-$9.82 (theoretical: $-$10.0) and a \qty{-3}{\dB} bandwidth of
\qty{72.3}{\kHz}. The Sallen--Key low-pass filter exhibited a roll-off of
\qty{-39.8}{\dB/decade}, consistent with a second-order Butterworth
response ($-40$~dB/decade theoretical). All results lie within component
tolerances ($\pm5\%$ resistors, $\pm10\%$ capacitors).

\section{Introduction and Theory}

\subsection{Inverting Amplifier}

The inverting amplifier, drawn in Figure~\ref{fig:invamp}, produces an
output $V_{\text{out}}$ that is an amplified, sign-inverted copy of the
input $V_{\text{in}}$. Under the ideal op-amp assumptions the closed-loop
gain is
\begin{equation}
  A_{v} \;=\; \frac{V_{\text{out}}}{V_{\text{in}}}
         \;=\; -\frac{R_{f}}{R_{i}}
  \label{eq:gain}
\end{equation}
where $R_f$ is the feedback resistor and $R_i$ the input resistor.  With
$R_f=\qty{100}{\kohm}$ and $R_i=\qty{10}{\kohm}$ the theoretical gain is
$A_v = -10.0$.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
  \centering
  \begin{circuitikz}[american,scale=0.95]
    \draw (0,1.8) node[op amp] (opamp) {};
    \draw (opamp.-) to[short] ++(-1.4,0) coordinate (sum)
          to[R,l_=$R_i$\;\qty{10}{\kohm}] ++(-2.6,0)
          to[short,-o] ++(-0.4,0) node[left]{$V_{\text{in}}$};
    \draw (sum) to[short,*-] ++(0,1.1) coordinate (fb)
          to[R,l=$R_f$\;\qty{100}{\kohm}]
          (fb -| opamp.out) to[short,-*] (opamp.out);
    \draw (opamp.+) to[short] ++(0,-0.9) node[ground]{};
    \draw (opamp.out) to[short,-o] ++(1,0) node[right]{$V_{\text{out}}$};
    \node at (2.2,-2.8) {};
  \end{circuitikz}
  \caption{Inverting amplifier.\;\;
    $R_i=\qty{10}{\kohm},\;R_f=\qty{100}{\kohm}\;\Rightarrow\;A_v=-10$.}
  \label{fig:invamp}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Active Low-Pass Filter}

A unity-gain Sallen--Key low-pass filter (Figure~\ref{fig:lpf}) yields a
second-order Butterworth response when $R_1=R_2=R$ and $C_1=2C_2$.  The
transfer function is
\begin{equation}
  H(s)=\frac{1}{s^{2}R^{2}C_{1}C_{2}+2sRC_{2}+1}
  \label{eq:tf}
\end{equation}
with a cut-off frequency
$f_c = \bigl(2\pi R\sqrt{C_{1}C_{2}}\,\bigr)^{-1}$.  Using
$R=\qty{10}{\kohm}$, $C_1=\qty{22}{\nF}$, and $C_2=\qty{10}{\nF}$
gives $f_c \approx \qty{1.07}{\kHz}$.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
  \centering
  \begin{circuitikz}[american,scale=0.95]
    \draw (0,0) node[op amp] (opamp) {};
    \draw (opamp.-) to[short] ++(0,1) to[short]
          (opamp.out |- {(0,1)}) to[short] (opamp.out);
    \draw (opamp.+) to[short] ++(-1.4,0) coordinate (mid)
          to[C,l_=$C_2$\;\qty{10}{\nF}] ++(0,-1.3) node[ground]{};
    \draw (mid) to[R,l_=$R_2$\;\qty{10}{\kohm}] ++(-2.4,0) coordinate (inj)
          to[R,l_=$R_1$\;\qty{10}{\kohm},-o] ++(-2,0)
          node[left]{$V_{\text{in}}$};
    \draw (inj) to[C,l=$C_1$\;\qty{22}{\nF}]
          (inj |- opamp.out) to[short,-*] (opamp.out);
    \draw (opamp.out) to[short,-o] ++(1,0) node[right]{$V_{\text{out}}$};
    \node at (2.2,-2.6) {};
  \end{circuitikz}
  \caption{Unity-gain Sallen--Key low-pass filter.\;\;
    $f_c\approx\qty{1.07}{\kHz}$.}
  \label{fig:lpf}
\end{figure}

\section{Experimental Setup}

\subsection{Equipment}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Oscilloscope:} Keysight DSOX1102G (70~MHz, 2~GSa/s)
  \item \textbf{DC Supply:} Agilent E3631A, $\pm\qty{15}{\V}$ rails
  \item \textbf{Signal Generator:} Rigol DG1022Z (25~MHz)
  \item \textbf{Op-Amp:} Texas Instruments LM741CN (DIP-8)
  \item \textbf{Passives:} $\pm5\%$ carbon-film resistors,
        $\pm10\%$ ceramic capacitors
  \item \textbf{Breadboard:} 830-point solderless, with
        \qty{22}{\AWG} jumpers
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Procedure}

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{\arabic*.},leftmargin=2.5em]
  \item Assemble the inverting amplifier of Figure~\ref{fig:invamp} on the
        breadboard. Verify $\pm\qty{15}{\V}$ DC rails with a multimeter before
        inserting the IC.
  \item Apply a \qty{500}{\mV} (peak-to-peak) sine wave at
        $f=\qty{100}{\Hz}$. Record $V_{\text{out}}$ on Channel~2 of the
        oscilloscope.
  \item Sweep the input through \qty{100}{\Hz}, \qty{1}{\kHz},
        \qty{10}{\kHz}, \qty{50}{\kHz}, and \qty{100}{\kHz}, recording
        $V_{\text{out}}$ at each step.
  \item Reconfigure for the Sallen--Key filter (Figure~\ref{fig:lpf}).
        Repeat the frequency sweep from \qty{100}{\Hz} to
        \qty{100}{\kHz} in 10 logarithmic steps.
  \item Export all oscilloscope screen captures as CSV for post-processing
        in Python.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{MATLAB / Octave Post-Processing}

Measured oscilloscope traces were exported as CSV and processed with the
script below.

\begin{lstlisting}[
  caption={Post-processing script for gain calculation.},
  label={lst:matlab}
]
% gain_analysis.m -- Compute gain from scope CSV export
data  = readtable('scope_trace.csv');
t     = data.Time_s;
vin   = data.Channel1_V;
vout  = data.Channel2_V;

% peak-to-peak detection
vin_pp   = max(vin)  - min(vin);
vout_pp  = max(vout) - min(vout);

gain     = vout_pp / vin_pp;
gain_dB  = 20 * log10(gain);

fprintf('Gain: %.2f  (%.1f dB)\n', gain, gain_dB);

% plot time-domain overlay for report figure
figure;
plot(t, vin, 'b-', 'LineWidth', 1.2); hold on;
plot(t, vout, 'r--', 'LineWidth', 1.2);
xlabel('Time (s)'); ylabel('Voltage (V)');
legend('V_{in}', 'V_{out}'); grid on;
\end{lstlisting}

  \textbf{Measurement Uncertainty:} All readings carry an uncertainty

  and $\pm\qty{5}{\percent}$ from resistor tolerance.  The combined RSS

\section{Discussion}

\subsection{Gain Accuracy}

The measured mid-band gain of $9.82$ deviates from the theoretical $10.0$
by \qty{-1.8}{\percent}.  The finite open-loop gain of the LM741
($A_{\text{OL}}\approx2\times10^{5}$ at DC) contributes a negligible error
--- the exact closed-loop gain,
\begin{equation}
  A_{v,\text{exact}}
    = -\frac{R_f/R_i}
           {1+\frac{1+R_f/R_i}{A_{\text{OL}}}}
    \approx -9.9995 ,
  \label{eq:exact}
\end{equation}
is dominated instead by resistor tolerance ($\pm5\%$ each) and parasitic
breadboard capacitance ($\sim\qty{3}{\pF}$ between adjacent rows).

\subsection{Bandwidth}

The \qty{-3}{\dB} bandwidth of \qty{72.3}{\kHz} is consistent with the
LM741's GBWP of \qty{1}{\MHz}.  For a noise gain of $11$, the expected
small-signal bandwidth is $f_{\text{GBWP}}/11 \approx
\qty{91}{\kHz}$; the observed reduction to \qty{72.3}{\kHz} reflects the
additional effect of slew-rate limiting ($\qty{0.5}{\V/\us}$ for the
LM741 at $\pm\qty{15}{\V}$ rails).

\subsection{Filter Performance}

The Sallen--Key pass-band is flat within \qty{0.1}{\dB} and the transition
band follows the predicted $-40$~dB/decade slope.  The \qty{4.7}{\percent}
deviation of $f_c$ falls within the combined capacitor tolerance
($\pm10\%$).  For precision applications, \qty{1}{\percent} metal-film
resistors and NP0/C0G capacitors are recommended.

\section{Conclusion}

Both op-amp circuits performed within expected tolerance.  The inverting
amplifier delivered a mid-band gain of $9.82$ (\qty{1.8}{\percent} error)
with a usable bandwidth of \qty{72.3}{\kHz}.  The Sallen--Key low-pass
filter achieved a \qty{-3}{\dB} cut-off at \qty{1.02}{\kHz} and the
canonical $-40$~dB/decade roll-off, confirming a clean second-order
Butterworth response.

The results validate the ideal op-amp model for frequencies well below the
gain-bandwidth limit and illustrate the practical consequences of finite
GBWP and slew rate in real devices.  Future work could explore a
rail-to-rail op-amp (e.g., OPA2340) for extended bandwidth, or cascade
two Sallen--Key stages for a fourth-order Butterworth alignment.
