\section{Background and Overview of Clinical Development Program}
\label{sec:background}
% ================================================================

Venlafaxine hydrochloride extended-release (VEN-ER) is a serotonin-norepinephrine reuptake
inhibitor (SNRI) approved in the United States (Effexor\textsuperscript{\textregistered} XR;
NDA\,20-699) for Major Depressive Disorder (MDD), generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), social
anxiety disorder (SAD), and panic disorder. This supplemental NDA (sNDA 22-614-S001) seeks
approval of a 150\,mg capsule strength manufactured at the sponsor's Cambridge, MA facility
(HLX-SITE-01), complementing the existing approved 37.5, 75, and 225\,mg strengths.

The clinical development program for the 150\,mg strength comprises:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=18pt, itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{HLX-BA-101} --- Bioavailability/bioequivalence (BE) study, fasting conditions
        ($n=48$, crossover; \modref{m5/5-1/hlx-ba-101.pdf}{m5/5.1/HLX-BA-101})
  \item \textbf{HLX-BA-102} --- BE study, fed conditions ($n=48$, crossover;
        \modref{m5/5-1/hlx-ba-102.pdf}{m5/5.1/HLX-BA-102})
  \item \textbf{HLX-MDD-301} --- Phase 3, randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled efficacy
        and safety study ($n=486$;
        \modref{m5/5-3-5/csr-hlx-mdd-301.pdf}{m5/5.3.5/HLX-MDD-301-CSR})
  \item \textbf{HLX-MDD-302} --- 52-week open-label safety extension ($n=412$;
        \modref{m5/5-3-5/csr-hlx-mdd-302.pdf}{m5/5.3.5/HLX-MDD-302-CSR})
\end{itemize}

The clinical program is supported by population pharmacokinetic (PopPK) modelling
(\modref{m2/2-7-2/pkwr.pdf}{m2/2.7.2/PopPK}) and a literature-based exposure--response
analysis (\modref{m2/2-7-2/erwr.pdf}{m2/2.7.2/ExposureResponse}).

\section{Overview of Biopharmaceutics and Clinical Pharmacology}
\label{sec:pk}
% ================================================================

\subsection{Bioequivalence --- Studies HLX-BA-101 and HLX-BA-102}
\label{sec:be}

Both pivotal BE studies employed a single-dose, two-period, two-sequence crossover design
in healthy adult subjects (18--55 years). The reference product was
Effexor\textsuperscript{\textregistered} XR 150\,mg (Wyeth LLC; US commercial stock).
Plasma concentrations of venlafaxine and its active metabolite O-desmethylvenlafaxine (ODV)
were measured by a validated LC-MS/MS assay
(\modref{m2/2-7-1/bioanreport.pdf}{m2/2.7.1/BioanalyticalReport}).

\subsection{Population Pharmacokinetics}
\label{sec:poppk}

A two-compartment PopPK model with first-order absorption and linear elimination was developed
using NONMEM\textsuperscript{\textregistered} v7.5 (\modref{m2/2-7-2/pkwr.pdf}{m2/2.7.2/PopPK}).
Key covariate effects: body weight on clearance (CL/F) and renal function (eGFR) on ODV
elimination. No dose adjustment is required for the 150\,mg strength based on exposure-matched
simulations across the approved adult population.

\section{Overview of Efficacy}
\label{sec:efficacy}
% ================================================================

\subsection{Pivotal Study HLX-MDD-301}
\label{sec:mdd301}

\subsubsection{Study Design}

HLX-MDD-301 was a 12-week, randomised (1:1), double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group
study in adult outpatients (18--65 years) meeting DSM-5 criteria for MDD with baseline MADRS
total score $\geq$\,28. Subjects were randomised to VEN-ER 150\,mg once daily (QD) or matching
placebo. The primary endpoint was change from baseline (CFB) in MADRS total score at Week\,8.

\subsubsection{Subgroup Analyses}

Consistent treatment effects were observed across pre-specified subgroups including age
($<$45 vs.\ $\geq$45 years), sex, race, baseline MADRS severity (severe: $\geq$34), and comorbid
anxiety (\modref{m5/5-3-5/csr-hlx-mdd-301.pdf}{m5/5.3.5/HLX-MDD-301 Figure\,14.2.5}). No
qualitative interactions were identified (all subgroup $p$-interaction\,$>$\,0.10).

\section{Overview of Safety}
\label{sec:safety}
% ================================================================

\subsection{Integrated Safety Population}

The integrated safety database comprises all subjects who received $\geq$1 dose of VEN-ER
150\,mg across HLX-MDD-301 and HLX-MDD-302 ($N$\,=\,655; total exposure 477 patient-years).

\begin{regwarn}
\textbf{Known Class Effects of SNRIs} (consistent with Effexor XR Reference Label, Section\,5):\\[3pt]
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=14pt, itemsep=1pt, topsep=0pt]
  \item Suicidality in young adults --- monitor closely during early treatment (\textit{Black Box Warning})
  \item Serotonin syndrome --- risk increased with concomitant serotonergic agents
  \item Elevated blood pressure --- dose-dependent; monitor BP before and during treatment
  \item Hyponatremia --- primarily in elderly; monitor serum sodium in at-risk patients
  \item Seizures, abnormal bleeding, activation of mania/hypomania
\end{itemize}
\end{regwarn}

\subsection{Serious Adverse Events and Deaths}

No deaths occurred during HLX-MDD-301 or HLX-MDD-302. Serious adverse events (SAEs) were
reported in 3\,(1.2\%) subjects in the VEN-ER group: 1 hospitalisation for hypertensive crisis
(resolved; assessed as not study-drug related), 1 appendectomy (unrelated), and 1 suicide
attempt (SUSAR reported per protocol;
\modref{m5/5-3-5/csr-hlx-mdd-301.pdf}{m5/5.3.5/SAE Narrative\,003}).
SAEs in placebo: 2\,(0.8\%).

\subsection{Discontinuations Due to Adverse Events}

Discontinuation due to AEs: VEN-ER 12.3\%; Placebo 5.3\% ($p=0.006$). Most common reasons
for VEN-ER discontinuation: nausea (3.7\%), dizziness (2.1\%), insomnia (1.6\%).

\section{Conclusions}
\label{sec:conclusions}
% ================================================================

The totality of data from the clinical development program supports approval of Venlafaxine
Hydrochloride Extended-Release Capsules 150\,mg for treatment of MDD in adults. The 150\,mg
strength is bioequivalent to Effexor\textsuperscript{\textregistered}\,XR, is effective
(primary and all key secondary endpoints met; $p<0.0001$), and is safe and well tolerated
consistent with the established class profile. No new or unexpected safety signals were
identified. The benefit-risk profile is favourable and the 150\,mg strength addresses a
clinically significant titration gap in the approved dosage regimen.
