\section{Introduction}

\subsection{Purpose and Scope}

This Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP) establishes the procedures, roles, and technical requirements for recovering critical IT systems and infrastructure operated by Nexa Financial Services. The plan covers the primary data centre located in Chicago, IL, and the secondary disaster recovery site in Phoenix, AZ.

The DRP applies to all production systems supporting the following business functions:
\begin{itemize}[nosep, leftmargin=*]
  \item Core banking and transaction processing
  \item Customer-facing digital platforms (web and mobile)
  \item Internal corporate systems (email, ERP, HRIS)
  \item Data warehousing and business intelligence
  \item Payment gateway and settlement services
\end{itemize}

\begin{infobox}
\textbf{\faInfoCircle\ Plan Activation Criteria}\\[4pt]
This plan is activated when the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO), in consultation with the Crisis Management Team, declares a disaster event. A disaster is defined as any unplanned event that results in the loss of critical IT services for more than \textbf{4 hours} or poses an imminent risk of extended service disruption.
\end{infobox}

\subsection{Definitions}

\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{@{}l X@{}}
  \textbf{RTO} & \textbf{Recovery Time Objective} --- the maximum acceptable duration of service interruption before recovery must be complete.\\
  \textbf{RPO} & \textbf{Recovery Point Objective} --- the maximum acceptable age of data that can be lost, measured from the point of failure.\\
  \textbf{BCP} & Business Continuity Plan.\\
  \textbf{DR Site} & The secondary facility in Phoenix, AZ, designated for disaster recovery operations.\\
  \textbf{MAAO} & Maximum Allowable Acceptable Outage --- the absolute ceiling on downtime beyond which the organisation faces existential harm.\\
\end{tabularx}

\subsection{Escalation Procedure}

\begin{enumerate}[nosep, leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Incident detected} --- Any team member or monitoring system identifies a potential disaster event.
  \item \textbf{Initial assessment} --- IT Recovery Lead assesses scope and impact within \textbf{15 minutes}.
  \item \textbf{Crisis Manager notification} --- If incident meets activation criteria, CISO is notified immediately.
  \item \textbf{Plan activation} --- CISO declares a disaster and activates this DRP.
  \item \textbf{Team assembly} --- All recovery team members report to the designated war room (physical: Chicago HQ, Conference Room B; virtual: secured bridge line).
  \item \textbf{Status updates} --- Communications Lead issues updates every \textbf{2 hours} to stakeholders.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Phase 4: Stabilisation and Return to Normal Operations}

\begin{infobox}
\textbf{\faClock\ Return to Primary Site}\\[4pt]
Once the primary Chicago data centre is fully restored and validated, a controlled failback is executed during a pre-scheduled maintenance window. The failback procedure mirrors the failover steps in reverse order, with an additional \textbf{2-hour} data synchronisation window between site deactivation at Phoenix and activation at Chicago.
\end{infobox}
