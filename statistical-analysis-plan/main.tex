\section{Introduction}

\subsection{Purpose of This Document}

This Statistical Analysis Plan (SAP) describes in detail the pre-specified statistical
methodology, analysis populations, estimands, and output specifications for the
primary, secondary, and exploratory analyses of Protocol \textbf{ARI-COPD-301}.
This document was finalised and locked \textit{prior to database lock and unblinding}
in accordance with ICH E9(R1) and the trial's pre-registration commitments.

\subsection{Protocol Synopsis}

\begin{tcolorbox}[sapnote, title={\faIcon{info-circle}\ Study at a Glance}]
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}>{\bfseries\color{sapblue}}l@{\hspace{8mm}}>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X@{}}
  Study Title       & CLARITY: A Phase III Trial of Arinestat vs.\ Placebo in COPD \\[2pt]
  Design            & Randomised, double-blind, parallel-group, placebo-controlled \\[2pt]
  Indication        & Moderate-to-severe COPD (GOLD Stage II–III) \\[2pt]
  Investigational Product & Arinestat 250\,\textmu g inhaled twice daily \\[2pt]
  Comparator        & Matching placebo inhaler twice daily \\[2pt]
  Randomisation     & 1:1, stratified by GOLD stage (II vs.\ III) and smoking status \\[2pt]
  Sample Size       & 620 participants (310 per arm) \\[2pt]
  Treatment Duration & 52 weeks (plus 4-week safety follow-up) \\[2pt]
  Primary Endpoint  & Change from baseline in trough FEV\textsubscript{1} at Week 52 \\[2pt]
  Key Sites         & 48 sites across USA, EU, Canada \\[2pt]
\end{tabularx}
\end{tcolorbox}

\section{Study Objectives and Estimands}

\subsection{Primary Objective}

To evaluate the efficacy of Arinestat 250\,\textmu g BID versus placebo in improving
pulmonary function in adults with moderate-to-severe COPD as measured by trough
forced expiratory volume in one second (FEV\textsubscript{1}) at Week 52.

\subsection{Secondary Objectives}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.8em, itemsep=3pt]
  \item To assess the effect of Arinestat on health-related quality of life (SGRQ Total Score).
  \item To evaluate the rate of moderate-to-severe COPD exacerbations over 52 weeks.
  \item To examine the effect on peak FEV\textsubscript{1} (2\,h post-dose) at Week 52.
  \item To determine the proportion of responders achieving $\geq$100\,mL improvement in
        trough FEV\textsubscript{1} at Week 52.
  \item To characterise the pharmacodynamic biomarker profile (serum fibronectin-1 levels).
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Estimand Framework (ICH E9R1)}

\begin{tcolorbox}[sapnote, title={\faIcon{flask}\ Estimand Definition — Primary Endpoint}]

  Variable            & Change from baseline in trough FEV\textsubscript{1} (litres) at Week 52 \\[2pt]
  Intervention        & Arinestat 250\,\textmu g BID vs.\ matching placebo BID \\[2pt]

\section{Primary Endpoint Analysis}

\subsection{Model Specification}

The primary analysis will use a Mixed Model for Repeated Measures (MMRM) fitted to
change from baseline in trough FEV\textsubscript{1} (L) at scheduled post-baseline visits
(Weeks 4, 12, 24, 40, 52).

\textbf{Model equation:}
\[
  Y_{ijk} = \mu + \alpha_i + \beta_j + (\alpha\beta)_{ij}
            + \gamma_1 \cdot \text{BL}_k + \gamma_2 \cdot \text{BL}_k \cdot V_j
            + \delta_1 \cdot \text{GOLD}_k + \delta_2 \cdot \text{SMOKE}_k + \varepsilon_{ijk}
\]

where $Y_{ijk}$ is the change from baseline for participant $k$ in treatment group $i$ at
visit $j$; $\alpha_i$ is the treatment effect; $\beta_j$ is the visit effect;
$(\alpha\beta)_{ij}$ is the treatment-by-visit interaction (the primary contrast at Week 52);
$\text{BL}_k$ is the baseline trough FEV\textsubscript{1}; $\text{GOLD}_k$ and
$\text{SMOKE}_k$ are stratification factors.

\subsection{Model Fitting Details}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.6em, itemsep=2pt]
  \item Covariance structure: unstructured (UN); if convergence fails, fall back to
        Toeplitz then compound symmetry, in that order.
  \item Degrees of freedom: Kenward--Roger approximation.
  \item Software: SAS\textsuperscript{\textregistered}\ PROC MIXED (v9.4).
  \item All post-baseline assessments included, regardless of adherence status
        (treatment-policy intercurrent event strategy).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Hypothesis and Decision Rule}

\[
  H_0{:}\ \mu_{\text{Arinestat}} - \mu_{\text{Placebo}} \leq 0
  \qquad\text{vs.}\qquad
  H_1{:}\ \mu_{\text{Arinestat}} - \mu_{\text{Placebo}} > 0
\]

A one-sided $\alpha = 0.025$ will be used (equivalent to two-sided $\alpha = 0.05$).
The primary endpoint will be declared statistically significant if the upper bound of
the two-sided 95\% CI for the treatment difference excludes zero and the one-sided
$p$-value $< 0.025$.

\subsection{Sensitivity Analyses}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.8em, label=\textbf{S\arabic*.}, itemsep=4pt]
  \item \textbf{Per-Protocol MMRM}: Repeat primary model in PP population.
  \item \textbf{ANCOVA at Week 52}: Single-visit ANCOVA using LOCF-imputed values as a
        pre-specified comparison; no alpha used.
  \item \textbf{Multiple Imputation (MI)}: Reference-based MI under missing-at-random (MAR)
        and missing-not-at-random (MNAR, jump-to-reference) assumptions; 1000 imputed
        datasets, Rubin's rules.
  \item \textbf{Tipping Point}: Compute the degree of MNAR departure needed to reverse
        significance.
\end{enumerate}

All confirmatory secondary endpoints will be tested using the fixed-sequence (hierarchical)
procedure outlined below. Testing proceeds to the next endpoint only if the preceding
test is significant at $\alpha = 0.025$ (one-sided).

\section{Safety Analyses}

Safety analyses will be performed in the Safety population using descriptive statistics
only; no formal hypothesis testing is pre-specified for safety endpoints.

\subsection{Adverse Events}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.6em, itemsep=2pt]
  \item Adverse events (AEs) are coded using MedDRA v27.0.
  \item Incidence of Treatment-Emergent AEs (TEAEs) summarised by System Organ Class
        (SOC) and Preferred Term (PT).
  \item Participants counted once per PT at the maximum severity and strongest relationship
        to study drug.
  \item Separate summaries for: all TEAEs, Grade $\geq$3 TEAEs, SAEs, TEAEs leading to
        discontinuation, AEs of Special Interest (AESI: cardiovascular, hepatic).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Clinical Laboratory Parameters}

Continuous lab parameters (haematology, biochemistry, urinalysis): descriptive statistics
(n, mean, SD, median, Q1/Q3, min, max) at each visit. Shift tables (normal/abnormal at
baseline vs.\ post-baseline) for key parameters. Notable abnormalities flagged using
CTCAE v5.0 grading and sponsor-defined reference ranges.

\subsection{Vital Signs and ECG}

Vital signs (blood pressure, heart rate, respiratory rate, SpO\textsubscript{2}) and
12-lead ECG parameters summarised descriptively. QTcF prolongation: proportion of
participants with QTcF $>$450, $>$480, $>$500\,ms and change from baseline $>$30, $>$60\,ms.

\begin{tcolorbox}[sapwarn, title={\faIcon{exclamation-triangle}\ Safety Review Committee Note}]
  An independent Safety Review Committee (SRC) will review unblinded safety data at
  Weeks 12 and 26. SRC findings may trigger an ad hoc safety analysis per the SRC
  Charter (Doc.\ ARI-301-SRC-001). Such analyses are not pre-specified in this SAP
  and will be reported separately.
\end{tcolorbox}

\section{Missing Data Handling}

\subsection{Assumed Missing Data Mechanism}

For the primary analysis, missing post-baseline FEV\textsubscript{1} values are assumed
to be Missing At Random (MAR) conditional on observed history, stratification factors,
and baseline covariates. This assumption is untestable but is clinically plausible given
the COPD context and observed withdrawal patterns.

\subsection{Primary Analysis}

The MMRM utilises all available observations; monotone and intermittent missingness are
both accommodated by the likelihood-based approach under MAR. No imputation is performed
in the primary analysis.

\subsection{Key ADEFF Derivations}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.6em, itemsep=2pt]
  \item \texttt{BASE}: Trough FEV\textsubscript{1} measured at Day 1 (Visit 2) pre-dose.
        If Day 1 value is missing, use Screening value.
  \item \texttt{CHG}: \texttt{AVAL} $-$ \texttt{BASE}.
  \item \texttt{ANL01FL}: \textquoteleft{}Y\textquoteright{} for records included in the
        primary MMRM (post-baseline scheduled visits, ITT population, no rescue medication
        within 48\,h of assessment).
  \item \texttt{AVISIT} mapping: Visits within a protocol-defined window are assigned to
        the nominal visit label (e.g., any assessment at Day 337$\pm$14 days $\rightarrow$
        ``Week 48''; Day 365$\pm$14 days $\rightarrow$ ``Week 52'').
\end{itemize}

\section{Sample Size and Power}

\subsection{Primary Endpoint Power Calculation}

Sample size was computed to detect a treatment difference of \textbf{80\,mL}
(0.080\,L) in change from baseline trough FEV\textsubscript{1} at Week 52, assuming:

\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}>{\color{sapblue}\bfseries}l@{\hspace{10mm}}>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X@{}}
\toprule
\rowcolor{sapfog}
\textbf{Parameter} & \textbf{Assumption} \\
\midrule
Clinically meaningful difference & 80\,mL (0.080\,L) \\
SD (within-group)                & 0.290\,L \\
Correlation (baseline, endpoint) & 0.55 \\
Type I error ($\alpha$, one-sided) & 0.025 \\
Power (1$-\beta$)                & 90\% \\
Dropout rate (Week 52)           & 20\% \\
Required per arm (completers)    & 248 \\
Required per arm (randomised)    & 310 \\
Total sample size                & 620 \\
\bottomrule
\multicolumn{2}{@{}l}{\footnotesize Computed using nQuery Advisor v8.5 (MMRM module, ANCOVA proxy).}
\end{tabularx}

\subsection{Interim Analyses}

No formal efficacy interim analyses are planned. One pre-specified futility-only interim
analysis will occur at approximately 50\% of events (approximately Week 52 of enrolment).
Futility boundary: conditional power $<15\%$ under the assumed effect size. The interim
is conducted by the independent Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) under the DSMB
Charter (Doc.\ ARI-301-DSMB-001). The sponsor remains blinded.
