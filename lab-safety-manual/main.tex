%% ── 1. Purpose & Scope ───────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Purpose \& Scope}

This Laboratory Safety Manual establishes minimum safety requirements for all personnel
conducting research, teaching, or support activities in Meridian BioSciences laboratories.
It applies to:

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep, topsep=2pt, leftmargin=1.4em]
  \item All full-time and part-time employees, including technicians and research associates.
  \item Graduate students, postdoctoral fellows, visiting scholars, and interns.
  \item Contractors and service engineers working inside laboratory spaces.
\end{itemize}

This document supplements but does not replace applicable federal, state, and local regulations,
including OSHA 29 CFR Part 1910 (General Industry), EPA 40 CFR Parts 260--270 (Hazardous
Waste), and NFPA 45 (Standard on Fire Protection for Laboratories Using Chemicals).

%% ── 3. General Lab Rules ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{General Laboratory Rules}

\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep, topsep=2pt, leftmargin=1.6em, label=\textcolor{accent}{\bfseries\arabic*.}]
  \item \textbf{No food or drink} is permitted in any laboratory space.
  \item \textbf{Closed-toe shoes and long trousers} are mandatory at all times.
  \item Working alone is \textbf{prohibited} when using acutely toxic, explosive, or highly reactive materials.
  \item Keep all \textbf{exits, aisles, and emergency equipment} clear at all times.
  \item \textbf{Label} every container: chemical name, concentration, hazard, preparer, and date.
  \item Never pipette by mouth; never smell or taste chemicals.
  \item Report \textbf{all injuries, spills, and near-misses} to the lab supervisor and EHS within 1 hour.
  \item Inspect equipment and glassware for defects before use; discard cracked glassware immediately.
  \item \textbf{Secure} compressed gas cylinders to a wall bracket or cart at all times.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{warnbox}
  Working in the laboratory outside normal hours (before 07:00 or after 19:00) requires written
  approval from your PI and a \textbf{buddy system}: a second person must be present or conducting
  remote check-ins every 30 minutes.
\end{warnbox}

\section{Chemical Hazards \& Storage}

\subsection{GHS Hazard Class Quick Reference}

\rowcolors{2}{rowshade}{white}
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}>{\bfseries}l >{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X@{}}
  \rowcolor{accent}
  \color{white}Hazard Class (GHS) & \color{white}Storage Requirements \\
  \midrule
  Flammable Liquids (Cat 1--2)   & Flammable storage cabinet; away from ignition sources; $\leq$10 L on bench. \\
  Corrosives (Acid/Base)         & Acid cabinet; acids and bases separated; secondary containment. \\
  Oxidisers                      & Isolated from organics and flammables; dedicated oxidiser cabinet. \\
  Acutely Toxic (Cat 1--3)       & Locked cabinet; access log required; quantities minimised to daily needs. \\
  Carcinogens / Mutagens         & Designated area with posted signage; fume hood use mandatory; SOPs required. \\
  Compressed Gases               & Chained upright; segregate oxidisers from flammables; protective caps on when not in use. \\
  Cryogenic Liquids              & Insulated Dewar vessels; never seal tightly; face shield and cryo gloves required. \\
  Pyrophorics                    & Strict written SOP; inert atmosphere; trained personnel only; EHS pre-approval required. \\
\end{tabularx}
\rowcolors{0}{}{}

\smallskip
\begin{dangerbox}
  \textbf{Incompatible chemicals must never be stored together.} Key incompatible pairs at
  Meridian: \textbf{Hydrofluoric acid} --- do not store with bases or in glass containers;
  \textbf{Perchloric acid} --- isolate from all organics; \textbf{Sodium azide} --- never contact
  copper, lead, or acid. Consult SOP-EHS-03 for the complete incompatibility matrix.
\end{dangerbox}

%% ── 6. Emergency Procedures ──────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Emergency Procedures}

\subsection{Chemical Spill}
\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep, topsep=2pt, leftmargin=1.6em, label=\textcolor{accent}{\bfseries\arabic*.}]
  \item \textbf{Assess} --- determine identity and quantity. If unknown or $>$1 L of hazardous chemical, \textbf{evacuate} and call EHS immediately.
  \item \textbf{Alert} --- notify others in the area; prevent re-entry.
  \item \textbf{Contain} --- for small spills ($<$1 L of known material), don appropriate PPE and use the nearest spill kit.
  \item \textbf{Clean up} --- absorb with inert material (vermiculite or spill pads); seal in a labelled container.
  \item \textbf{Decontaminate} --- clean the area thoroughly; remove and bag contaminated PPE.
  \item \textbf{Report} --- complete an incident report on the EHS portal within 24 hours.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Fire --- RACE Protocol}
\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep, topsep=2pt, leftmargin=1.6em, label=\textcolor{accent}{\bfseries\arabic*.}]
  \item \textbf{Rescue} anyone in immediate danger (only if safe to do so).
  \item \textbf{Alarm} --- pull the nearest fire alarm; call \textbf{911}.
  \item \textbf{Contain} --- close all doors and windows to limit spread.
  \item \textbf{Evacuate} --- use nearest stairwell (no elevators); assemble at \textbf{East Parking Lot, Zone B}.
  \item Use a fire extinguisher (PASS: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep) only for small, contained fires, and only if trained.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{First Aid \& Injury}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep, topsep=2pt, leftmargin=1.4em]
  \item \textbf{Eye exposure:} Flush immediately at the nearest eyewash station for \textbf{15 minutes minimum}; seek medical evaluation.
  \item \textbf{Skin contact:} Remove contaminated clothing; flush with water for 15 minutes at the safety shower; seek medical evaluation for corrosive or toxic materials.
  \item \textbf{Inhalation:} Move to fresh air; call 911 if person is unconscious or shows respiratory distress.
  \item \textbf{Ingestion:} Call Poison Control at \textbf{1-800-222-1222}; do not induce vomiting unless directed; call 911 for life-threatening exposure.
\end{itemize}

%% ── 7. Waste Disposal ────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Waste Disposal}

All chemical waste must be managed in accordance with EPA 40 CFR and applicable state
regulations. Key requirements:

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep, topsep=2pt, leftmargin=1.4em]
  \item Accumulate waste in \textbf{compatible, closed containers} labelled ``Hazardous Waste'',
    with full chemical name, concentration, and hazard class.
  \item \textbf{Never} pour chemicals down the drain without written EHS authorisation.
  \item Satellite accumulation containers must remain $\leq$55 gallons and be stored at the point of generation.
  \item Request waste pickup via the \textbf{EHS Waste Portal} (\texttt{waste.meridian.internal}) when containers reach 75\% capacity.
  \item Biological waste must be \textbf{autoclaved} or placed in designated biohazard bags before disposal.
  \item Sharps (needles, blades, broken glass) must go in \textbf{puncture-resistant sharps containers}; contact EHS for disposal.
\end{itemize}

\begin{warnbox}[title={\faExclamationTriangle\enskip WARNING --- Drain Disposal}]
  Disposing of hazardous chemicals via the sink or drain without authorisation is a
  \textbf{federal violation} subject to fines exceeding \$37{,}500 per day per violation.
  When in doubt, treat it as hazardous waste.
\end{warnbox}
