\section{Financial Overview \& Revenue Growth}
Consolidated revenue for Q3 2026 reached \$248.5 million, up from \$220.0 million in the prior-year period. This steady growth highlights the strength of our enterprise offerings and resilience in key markets.
\par
The primary growth driver was \textbf{Nexa Infrastructure}, which expanded its cloud hosting capacity, generating \$112.5 million. \textbf{Synapse Solutions} also demonstrated solid expansion, bringing in \$76.2 million through high-margin software-as-a-service (SaaS) renewals. \textbf{Vertex Logistics} experienced modest growth of 8.3\%, posting \$41.8 million despite global freight market volatility. Other segments remained flat at \$18.0 million.

\section{Operating Margins \& Expense Analysis}
Consolidated EBITDA stood at \$62.3 million, yielding an EBITDA margin of 25.1\%. This reflects positive operating leverage, particularly in our infrastructure division.
\par
Operating expenses (OPEX) totaled \$186.2 million, which includes \$4.5 million in cost-integration charges related to the acquisition of \textbf{Nimbus Technologies}. Research \& Development (R\&D) investments grew to \$18.4 million, focusing on Synapse's AI auditing platform. Sales \& Marketing expenses stabilized at \$22.1 million.

\section{Balance Sheet \& Liquidity Analysis}
Meridian's balance sheet remains highly resilient. Total cash and cash equivalents stood at \$112.0 million at the end of the quarter. Operating cash flow was \$52.8 million, while free cash flow reached \$44.2 million. The slight decline in free cash flow was expected, reflecting accelerated capital expenditures in the Vertex Cloud facility.

\section{Strategic Capital Expenditures}
Capital expenditures in Q3 totaled \$32.4 million, with 70\% allocated to high-yield expansion projects. The primary investment remains the expansion of Nexa's green data center in the Northern Region, which is on track to become operational in Q1 2027. Additionally, Meridian commenced a \$12.0 million equity investment in \textbf{Apex Technologies} to secure critical supply lines.
