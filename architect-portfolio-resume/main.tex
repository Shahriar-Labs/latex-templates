  {\large\bfseries\color{dark}Selected Projects}
  \sectionrule

  \begin{projectbox}
    {\bfseries Riverside Cultural Center} \hfill {\color{medium}2020 -- 2023}\\[2pt]
    {\color{accent}Portland, OR \textbar{} 45,000 sq ft \textbar{} \$32M Budget}\\[5pt]
    A contemporary cultural institution situated along the Willamette River featuring a cantilevered glass facade that frames panoramic views of the waterfront. The design integrates a 300-seat performance hall, three gallery spaces, and a public research library within a fluid, interconnected floor plan. Achieved LEED Platinum certification through innovative passive cooling strategies, a living roof system, and locally sourced timber structure. Received the 2023 AIA Design Excellence Award.
  \end{projectbox}

  \begin{projectbox}
    {\bfseries The Paragon Tower} \hfill {\color{medium}2018 -- 2022}\\[2pt]
    {\color{accent}Seattle, WA \textbar{} 32 Stories \textbar{} 380,000 sq ft \textbar{} \$145M Budget}\\[5pt]
    A 32-story mixed-use high-rise integrating residential, retail, and public amenity spaces in Seattle's South Lake Union district. The tower's distinctive faceted facade responds to solar orientation, reducing thermal gain by 35\% while creating dynamic interior light conditions. Vertical gardens at six intermediate levels provide shared outdoor terraces and support the building's net-zero water strategy. The project anchors a three-block master plan developed in collaboration with the City of Seattle's Office of Planning.
  \end{projectbox}

  \begin{projectbox}
    {\bfseries Harborview Plaza} \hfill {\color{medium}2019 -- 2021}\\[2pt]
    {\color{accent}San Diego, CA \textbar{} 2.4 acres \textbar{} Public Realm}\\[5pt]
    Waterfront revitalization project transforming a former industrial pier into an award-winning public plaza and community gathering space. The design features a series of terraced seating platforms, an interactive water feature, and a shade canopy structure inspired by the region's maritime heritage. Native drought-tolerant landscaping and permeable paving systems manage 100\% of stormwater on-site. The project has become a catalyst for adjacent private development, generating \$200M+ in follow-on investment.
  \end{projectbox}

  \begin{projectbox}
    {\bfseries Stonefield Residences} \hfill {\color{medium}2016 -- 2019}\\[2pt]
    {\color{accent}Austin, TX \textbar{} 24 Units \textbar{} 52,000 sq ft \textbar{} \$11M Budget}\\[5pt]
    Boutique residential complex of 24 luxury units organized around a central courtyard garden. The design draws from the regional vernacular of Texas Hill Country limestone construction, reinterpreting traditional massing with contemporary detailing and expansive glazing. Each unit features a private outdoor terrace and cross-ventilated floor plan. The project achieved Austin Energy Green Building 4-Star rating and was published in Dwell and Architect Magazine.
  \end{projectbox}
