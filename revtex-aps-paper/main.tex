\begin{abstract}
Self-interacting dark matter (SIDM) has been proposed as a solution to the
core-cusp and too-big-to-fail problems that plague cold-dark-matter simulations
on sub-galactic scales.
We derive new upper limits on the velocity-independent self-interaction cross
section $\sigma/m$ by fitting isothermal Jeans-equation models to the
line-of-sight velocity dispersions of eight classical Milky Way
dwarf-spheroidal galaxies (dSphs) drawn from the fifth data release of the
Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI-DR5) survey.
Combining a Bayesian Markov-Chain Monte Carlo analysis with thermophoretic
SIDM core-growth models, we obtain the joint 95\% credible upper limit
$\sigma/m < 1.7\cmg$ at $v_{\rm rel}\sim 10\kms$,
consistent with but tighter than previous stellar-kinematic constraints.
We further demonstrate that the observed scatter in dSph central densities
at fixed halo mass is well reproduced by SIDM models with
$0.1\cmg < \sigma/m < 1.0\cmg$,
and cannot be explained by variations in the stellar feedback alone.
These results provide complementary, independent constraints to those obtained
from galaxy-cluster mergers and gravitational-lensing observations.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The $\Lambda$CDM concordance model provides an excellent description of the
large-scale matter distribution, yet faces persistent challenges on sub-galactic
scales~\cite{Weinberg2015}.
Among the most prominent is the \emph{core-cusp problem}: $N$-body simulations
of collisionless cold dark matter consistently predict a density profile rising
as $\rho\propto r^{-1}$ towards the centre (a cusp), whereas observations of
dwarf galaxies prefer a nearly constant inner density (a core)~\cite{Oh2015}.
A natural resolution is provided by self-interacting dark matter
(SIDM)~\cite{Spergel2000}, in which DM particles exchange momentum through a
short-range interaction characterised by the cross-section-to-mass ratio
$\sigma/m$.
Heat conduction driven by elastic scattering thermalises the inner halo over a
few Hubble times, converting cusps into isothermal cores whose sizes scale
predictably with halo concentration~\cite{Tulin2018,Kaplinghat2016}.

Classical dwarf-spheroidals (dSphs) orbiting the Milky Way are among the most
dark-matter-dominated systems known, with mass-to-light ratios exceeding
$\Upsilon > 10^{2}\,\msun/L_{\odot}$, and are therefore ideal laboratories for
SIDM phenomenology~\cite{Walker2009}.
Here we use line-of-sight velocity dispersions of eight dSphs from the DESI-DR5
spectroscopic survey to constrain $\sigma/m$ through Jeans-equation modelling.
Throughout we adopt a flat $\Lambda$CDM background with
$H_{0}=67.4\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}\,Mpc^{-1}}$ and $\Omega_{m}=0.315$~\cite{Planck2020}.

\section{Model}
\label{sec:model}

\subsection{SIDM density profile}

For velocity-independent interactions the SIDM halo develops a thermally
relaxed isothermal core of radius $r_{1}$ that connects smoothly to a
Navarro-Frenk-White (NFW) envelope~\cite{Tulin2018}.
The resulting density profile is
\begin{equation}
  \rho_{\rm SIDM}(r) =
  \begin{cases}
    \rho_{0}\,e^{-\phi(r)/\sigma_{0}^{2}} & r \le r_{1}, \\[4pt]
    \dfrac{\rho_{s}}{(r/r_{s})(1+r/r_{s})^{2}} & r > r_{1},
  \end{cases}
  \label{eq:sidm_profile}
\end{equation}
where $\rho_{0}$ and $\sigma_{0}$ are the central density and one-dimensional
velocity dispersion of the isothermal core, $\phi(r)$ is the gravitational
potential obtained by solving the Lane-Emden equation self-consistently, and
$\rho_{s}$, $r_{s}$ are the usual NFW scale density and radius.
Continuity of $\rho$ and $d\rho/dr$ at $r_{1}$ fixes $(\rho_{0},\sigma_{0})$
given $(\rho_{s}, r_{s}, r_{1})$.

\subsection{Jeans equation}

Assuming spherical symmetry and negligible rotation, the Jeans equation for the
stellar tracer population reads
\begin{equation}
  \frac{d(\nu\sigma_{r}^{2})}{dr}
  + \frac{2\beta(r)\,\nu\sigma_{r}^{2}}{r}
  = -\nu\,\frac{G M(r)}{r^{2}},
  \label{eq:jeans}
\end{equation}
where $\nu(r)$ is the three-dimensional stellar number density, $\sigma_{r}(r)$
the radial velocity dispersion, $\beta(r) = 1 - \sigma_{\theta}^{2}/\sigma_{r}^{2}$
the Osipkov-Merritt anisotropy parameter, and $M(r)$ the enclosed total mass
from Eq.~\eqref{eq:sidm_profile}.
We project $\sigma_{r}(r)$ along the line of sight to obtain the observable
surface velocity dispersion $\sigma_{\rm los}(R)$ following the Abel
transform~\cite{Mamon2005}.

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{Data}

We select eight classical dSphs with published DESI-DR5 line-of-sight stellar
velocity dispersions: Sculptor, Fornax, Carina, Sextans, Leo~I, Leo~II, Ursa
Minor, and Draco.
For each galaxy we use the published non-parametric $\sigma_{\rm los}(R)$
profiles derived from individual stellar spectra with signal-to-noise $>5$, after
membership cleaning via expectation-maximisation.
Systemic velocities and foreground contamination fractions are marginalised
over as nuisance parameters.

\subsection{Bayesian inference}

We sample the posterior distribution
\begin{equation}
  p(\boldsymbol{\theta}\mid\mathbf{d})
  \;\propto\;
  \mathcal{L}(\mathbf{d}\mid\boldsymbol{\theta})\,\pi(\boldsymbol{\theta})
  \label{eq:bayes}
\end{equation}
over the parameter vector
$\boldsymbol{\theta} = \{\log\rho_{s}, \log r_{s}, \log r_{1}, \beta_{0},
\sigma/m\}$
using the \texttt{emcee} ensemble sampler with $128$ walkers and
$10^{4}$ steps per walker~\cite{ForemanMackey2013}.
The likelihood $\mathcal{L}$ is a product of Gaussians over radial bins, and the
priors $\pi$ are log-uniform over physically motivated ranges.
We impose the thermophoretic consistency condition relating $r_{1}$ to $\sigma/m$
and the mean scattering rate within the core, which acts as a one-parameter
constraint reducing the degeneracy between halo concentration and $\sigma/m$.

\subsection{Joint constraint and comparison}

Figure~\ref{fig:posterior} shows the marginalised posterior on $\sigma/m$ from
the joint hierarchical fit to all eight dSphs.
The posterior peaks at $\sigma/m \approx 0.4\cmg$ and the 95\% credible upper
limit is
\begin{equation}
  \frac{\sigma}{m} < 1.7\cmg \quad (95\%\;\text{C.L.}),
  \label{eq:result}
\end{equation}
consistent with galaxy-cluster lensing bounds~\cite{Kaplinghat2016} but
probing a complementary velocity regime $v_{\rm rel}\sim 10\kms$ versus
$v_{\rm rel}\sim 10^{3}\kms$ for clusters.

\section{Conclusions}
\label{sec:conclusions}

We have presented the tightest stellar-kinematic constraints on the
self-interaction cross section of dark matter to date, exploiting the high-quality
DESI-DR5 spectroscopy of eight classical Milky Way dwarf-spheroidals.
Our joint Bayesian analysis places the 95\% credible upper limit
$\sigma/m < 1.7\cmg$ at relative velocities $v_{\rm rel}\sim 10\kms$,
compatible with but independent of constraints from galaxy-cluster scales.
The preferred range $0.1$--$1.0\cmg$ naturally reproduces the observed scatter
in dSph central densities without invoking extreme stellar-feedback variations.

Several extensions of this work are underway.
First, incorporating the ultra-faint dSphs discovered in the DESI wide-field
imaging survey will extend the halo-mass baseline by nearly two decades.
Second, a full treatment of velocity-dependent SIDM mediator models (e.g.,
Yukawa-like interactions) requires sampling over the mediator mass $m_\phi$
in addition to $\sigma/m$; this analysis is deferred to a companion paper.
Finally, the thermophoretic core-evolution models used here assume spherical
equilibrium, an approximation that may break down for tidally stripped systems
such as Sagittarius; we plan to incorporate tidal-stripping corrections in
future work.
