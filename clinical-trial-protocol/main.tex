\section{Background and Rationale}
\label{sec:background}
% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Disease Context}

Non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) remains the leading cause of cancer-related mortality worldwide, accounting for approximately 1.8 million deaths annually. Despite transformative advances in immune checkpoint inhibitor (ICI) therapy and targeted agents, patients whose tumours harbour \textit{KRAS} mutations --- representing 25--30\% of all lung adenocarcinomas --- have historically lacked effective molecularly targeted options.

The \textit{KRAS} G12C substitution is the most prevalent oncogenic \textit{KRAS} allele in NSCLC ($\sim$13\% of cases). The landmark approval of sotorasib in 2021 validated KRAS G12C as a druggable target; however, median PFS of 6.8 months and an ORR of 37\% in the second-line setting underscore the need for more durable strategies.

\subsection{Velanixib (VNX-1803)}

Velanixib is a novel, potent, highly selective, orally bioavailable covalent inhibitor of KRAS G12C (IC\textsubscript{50} = 0.42\,nM, SOS1-displacement biochemical assay). Preclinical studies demonstrate nanomolar growth inhibition in KRAS G12C-positive cell lines (NCI-H23, MIA PaCa-2) and durable tumour regression in xenograft models. Importantly, VNX-1803 achieves brain-penetrant concentrations ($K_{\mathrm{p,uu,brain}}$ = 0.38), addressing a major unmet need given the 30--50\% incidence of CNS metastases in advanced NSCLC.

Phase I dose-escalation (VNX-NSCLC-101; $n=48$) established the recommended Phase II dose (RP2D) of 240\,mg QD, with a manageable safety profile (dose-limiting toxicity: Grade 3 fatigue at 360\,mg in 2 patients). Preliminary efficacy data yielded an ORR of 44\% and a 12-month OS rate of 68\% in KRAS G12C NSCLC.

\subsection{Scientific Rationale for Combination}

Accumulating evidence supports synergistic interaction between KRAS inhibition and PD-1 blockade:

\begin{itemize}
  \item KRAS G12C inhibition upregulates MHC-I surface expression and increases tumour-infiltrating lymphocyte density in preclinical models.
  \item Immune evasion via KRAS-driven MAPK pathway suppresses interferon-$\gamma$ signalling; VNX-1803 reverses this immunosuppressive phenotype.
  \item Clinical data from the CodeBreaK 101 study suggest sotorasib plus pembrolizumab is feasible, though hepatotoxicity requires monitoring.
  \item VNX-1803 exhibits a differentiated hepatic safety profile (ALT elevation $>3\times$ULN: 6\% vs.\ 23\% for sotorasib) and is hypothesised to enable safer combination use.
\end{itemize}

\begin{protocolnote}{\faInfoCircle\quad Scientific Premise}
  The KITE-II study tests the hypothesis that dual blockade of the KRAS G12C oncogenic driver and the PD-1/PD-L1 immune checkpoint will produce superior and more durable responses compared with either agent alone in a biomarker-selected NSCLC population.
\end{protocolnote}

\section{Study Design}
\label{sec:design}
% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Overview}

KITE-II is a Phase II, open-label, single-arm, multi-centre study enrolling approximately 82 patients across $\sim$18 investigational sites. There is no randomisation or blinding of investigators; however, tumour response assessment will be performed by a blinded independent central review (BICR) committee to minimise ascertainment bias.

\subsection{Treatment Regimen}

Eligible patients will receive:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Velanixib (VNX-1803):} 240\,mg orally, once daily (QD), continuously in 21-day cycles.
  \item \textbf{Pembrolizumab:} 200\,mg IV over 30 minutes on Day 1 of each 21-day cycle.
\end{itemize}

Treatment continues until radiographic progression per RECIST v1.1 (confirmed by BICR), unacceptable toxicity, patient withdrawal, or a maximum of 24 months (pembrolizumab), whichever occurs first. Velanixib may continue beyond 24 months at investigator discretion if clinical benefit is maintained.

\subsection{Study Periods}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Screening:} Up to 28 days prior to Cycle 1 Day 1 (C1D1).
  \item \textbf{Treatment Period:} C1D1 until end of treatment (EoT).
  \item \textbf{Safety Follow-Up:} 30-day safety visit after last dose.
  \item \textbf{Survival Follow-Up:} Minimum 12 months post-EoT (Q12W contacts).
\end{enumerate}

\section{Eligibility Criteria}
\label{sec:eligibility}
% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Inclusion Criteria}

Patients must meet \textbf{all} of the following:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Histologically or cytologically confirmed advanced or metastatic NSCLC (Stage IIIB--IVB per AJCC 8th ed.), predominantly non-squamous histology.
  \item Documented \textit{KRAS} G12C mutation by a validated local or central NGS or PCR assay.
  \item Receipt of at least one prior platinum-containing chemotherapy regimen for advanced/metastatic disease; prior immunotherapy permitted if not within 6 months.
  \item Age $\geq$18 years at time of informed consent.
  \item ECOG Performance Status 0 or 1.
  \item Measurable disease per RECIST v1.1 (target lesion $\geq$10\,mm in longest diameter, or $\geq$15\,mm for lymph nodes in short axis).
  \item Adequate organ function within 10 days prior to C1D1:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item ANC $\geq 1.5 \times 10^9$/L; Haemoglobin $\geq$9\,g/dL; Platelets $\geq 100 \times 10^9$/L.
      \item Creatinine $\leq$1.5$\times$ULN or GFR $\geq$50\,mL/min/1.73\,m\textsuperscript{2}.
      \item AST and ALT $\leq$2.5$\times$ULN ($\leq$5$\times$ULN if hepatic metastases).
      \item Total bilirubin $\leq$1.5$\times$ULN (Gilbert syndrome: $\leq$3$\times$ULN).
    \end{itemize}
  \item Female patients of childbearing potential: negative serum pregnancy test within 72 hours of C1D1; agree to highly effective contraception during treatment and 6 months after last dose.
  \item Ability to swallow oral medication.
  \item Written informed consent prior to any study-specific procedure.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Exclusion Criteria}

Patients must not meet \textbf{any} of the following:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Known activating \textit{EGFR} mutation (exon 19 del or L858R) or \textit{ALK}/\textit{ROS1}/\textit{RET}/\textit{MET} exon 14 alteration qualifying for approved targeted therapy.
  \item Prior treatment with any KRAS G12C inhibitor.
  \item Uncontrolled or symptomatic brain metastases. (Stable, treated CNS metastases off steroids $\geq$14 days are eligible.)
  \item Active autoimmune disease requiring systemic treatment within 2 years. Replacement therapies are permitted.
  \item Systemic immunosuppressive agents within 14 days of C1D1.
  \item History of pneumonitis requiring systemic steroids, or current non-infectious pneumonitis.
  \item Active hepatitis B (HBsAg+) or hepatitis C (HCV RNA detectable).
  \item Known HIV with CD4 $<$350 cells/$\mu$L or detectable viral load within 6 months.
  \item Concurrent malignancy (except adequately treated basal/squamous cell skin carcinoma or cervical carcinoma in situ).
  \item Significant cardiovascular disease: uncontrolled hypertension (BP $>$160/100), NYHA Class III/IV HF, MI within 6 months, QTcF $>$470\,ms (female) or $>$450\,ms (male).
  \item Major surgery within 28 days prior to C1D1.
  \item Pregnancy or breast-feeding.
  \item Any condition that in the investigator's judgement would make the patient unsuitable for participation.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Statistical Considerations}
\label{sec:stats}
% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Sample Size and Power}

The primary endpoint is ORR assessed by BICR. Based on published Phase II data for KRAS G12C-targeted agents:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Null hypothesis (H\textsubscript{0}):} ORR $\leq$25\% (historical second-line docetaxel).
  \item \textbf{Alternative hypothesis (H\textsubscript{1}):} ORR $\geq$45\% (anticipated for the combination).
  \item \textbf{Statistical test:} One-sample exact binomial test (one-sided $\alpha$ = 0.05).
  \item \textbf{Power:} $\geq$85\% to reject H\textsubscript{0} if true ORR = 45\%.
  \item \textbf{Sample size:} 62 evaluable patients; $\sim$82 enrolled (25\% screen failure).
\end{itemize}

\begin{protocolnote}{\faChartBar\quad Sample Size Note}
  Calculated using the exact binomial method (nQuery Advisor v10.0; one-sided $\alpha=0.05$, power $=85\%$, $p_0=0.25$, $p_1=0.45$). Sensitivity analysis confirms $n=62$ yields $>$90\% power if true ORR $=50\%$.
\end{protocolnote}

\subsection{Statistical Methods}

\textbf{Primary endpoint (ORR):} The point estimate and exact 95\% Clopper-Pearson confidence interval will be computed. A one-sided one-sample exact binomial test will be conducted at $\alpha=0.05$. H\textsubscript{0} will be rejected if the lower bound of the 95\% CI exceeds 25\%.

\textbf{Time-to-event endpoints:} PFS, OS, and DoR will be estimated using the Kaplan-Meier method. Medians and 95\% CIs (Brookmeyer-Crowley method) will be reported. Landmark analyses at 6, 12, and 18 months will be performed.

\textbf{Subgroup analyses:} Pre-specified subgroup analyses will explore ORR by PD-L1 TPS ($<$1\%, 1--49\%, $\geq$50\%), prior immunotherapy history, CNS metastases, and ctDNA kinetics. These are considered exploratory.

\textbf{Missing data:} No imputation for the primary endpoint (non-responders classified as failures). Sensitivity analyses with per-protocol and worst-case imputation will be conducted for secondary endpoints.

\section{Safety and Adverse Event Reporting}
\label{sec:safety}
% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Adverse Event Definitions}

All AEs will be graded per \textbf{NCI CTCAE Version 5.0}.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Adverse Event (AE):} Any untoward medical occurrence, not necessarily causally related to study treatment.
  \item \textbf{Serious Adverse Event (SAE):} Any AE resulting in: death; life-threatening condition; new/prolonged hospitalisation; persistent significant disability; congenital anomaly; or medically significant event.
  \item \textbf{Dose-Limiting Toxicity (DLT):} As defined in the Phase I VNX-NSCLC-101 study and the Investigator's Brochure (Section 7.3).
  \item \textbf{Adverse Event of Special Interest (AESI):} Immune-related AEs (irAEs), hepatotoxicity (ALT/AST $\geq 3\times$ULN), interstitial lung disease/pneumonitis, QTc prolongation $>$60\,ms from baseline or absolute $>$500\,ms.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{SAE Reporting Timelines}

\begin{safetynote}{\faExclamationTriangle\quad Mandatory SAE Reporting Requirements}
  \begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.2em]
    \item \textbf{Fatal or Life-Threatening SAEs:} Report to Sponsor Medical Monitor within \textbf{24 hours}. Initial report may be verbal; written follow-up required.
    \item \textbf{All other SAEs:} Report to Sponsor within \textbf{72 hours} using the study SAE form.
    \item \textbf{Sponsor-to-Regulatory (SUSARs):} Fatal/life-threatening: \textbf{7 calendar days}; all others: \textbf{15 calendar days} (per 21 CFR 312.32; EudraVigilance).
    \item \textbf{IRB/IEC:} Per local requirements, typically within 10 business days.
    \item \textbf{DSMB:} All Grade 4--5 AEs regardless of attribution within \textbf{14 days}.
  \end{itemize}
\end{safetynote}

\subsection{Dose Modifications}

\subsubsection{Velanixib Dose Levels}

Dose levels: Level 0 (starting) 240\,mg QD; Level $-$1: 160\,mg QD; Level $-$2: 80\,mg QD. No re-escalation permitted after reduction.

\begin{itemize}
  \item Grade 2 persisting $>$7 days: Interrupt until $\leq$ Grade 1; restart at same dose.
  \item Grade 3 (first): Interrupt; restart at Level $-$1 after recovery.
  \item Grade 3 (second) or Grade 4: Permanently discontinue velanixib.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Pembrolizumab}

No dose reduction scheme; pembrolizumab is withheld or permanently discontinued per irAE grade (Keytruda\textsuperscript{\textregistered} USPI and site-specific irAE management guidelines):

\begin{itemize}
  \item Grade 2 irAE: Withhold; restart when $\leq$ Grade 1 and steroids $\leq$10\,mg prednisone equivalent.
  \item Grade 3 irAE: Withhold + high-dose corticosteroids (1--2\,mg/kg). Permanently discontinue if not improved to $\leq$ Grade 1 within 12 weeks.
  \item Grade 4 irAE (non-endocrinopathy): Permanently discontinue pembrolizumab.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB)}

An independent DSMB (three oncologists, one biostatistician, one patient advocate) will conduct scheduled safety reviews after every 20 enrolled patients and after the first 40 patients complete Cycle 3. Pre-specified stopping rules:

\begin{itemize}
  \item $\geq$5 Grade 5 AEs considered at least possibly treatment-related.
  \item $\geq$30\% incidence of Grade $\geq$3 hepatotoxicity (ALT/AST $\geq 5\times$ULN confirmed).
  \item $\geq$20\% incidence of Grade $\geq$3 pneumonitis.
\end{itemize}

\section{Ethics and Informed Consent}
\label{sec:ethics}
% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Ethical Principles}

This study will be conducted in full compliance with:
\begin{itemize}
  \item The Declaration of Helsinki (Fortaleza 2013 revision).
  \item ICH E6(R2) Good Clinical Practice guidelines.
  \item ICH E8(R1) General Considerations for Clinical Studies.
  \item 21 CFR Parts 50, 56, and 312 (USA); EU Clinical Trials Regulation No.\ 536/2014.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{IRB/IEC Approval}

The final protocol, ICF, and all patient-facing materials must receive written approval from the accredited IRB/IEC at each site prior to any study-specific procedures. Protocol amendments must be approved before implementation unless necessary to eliminate an immediate safety hazard.

\subsection{Informed Consent Process}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item The investigator or delegated, trained team member will explain the study in full in a private setting, using plain-language materials appropriate to the patient's comprehension level.
  \item Adequate time (minimum 24 hours) will be provided for the patient to consider participation; involvement of family or an independent advocate is encouraged.
  \item Written informed consent must be obtained \textbf{before} any study-specific procedure, including eligibility blood or tissue collection.
  \item For patients with diminished decision-making capacity, consent of a legally authorised representative will be sought per applicable law.
  \item Patients may withdraw at any time without penalty or loss of otherwise-entitled benefit.
  \item Re-consent is required for amendments affecting patient participation, risk, or data collection.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Data Protection and Confidentiality}

Patient data will be coded by unique patient identification number; no identifying information will be transmitted to the Sponsor. Data will be processed in accordance with GDPR (EU 2016/679) and HIPAA (45 CFR Parts 160 and 164) as applicable, and stored in a validated, access-controlled EDC system (Medidata Rave, Version 6.x).

\subsection{Publication Policy}

Results will be submitted for peer-reviewed publication within 12 months of primary data lock, regardless of outcome, per ICMJE guidelines. All investigators contributing $\geq$3 enrolled patients will review manuscripts prior to submission.

Amendments will be formalised as numbered changes requiring Sponsor, Medical Monitor, and IRB/IEC approval (unless urgent safety measures). All amendments will be tracked in the Protocol Amendment Log (Appendix E).

\subsection{Investigator Responsibilities}

Each principal investigator (PI) is responsible for: conducting the study per protocol, GCP, and applicable regulations; maintaining delegation logs, training records, and site staff CVs; accurate timely EDC data entry; IP storage and dispensing per the IMP management plan; and immediate SAE and protocol deviation reporting to the Sponsor.

\subsection{Record Retention}

All study documents (source data, signed ICFs, IP accountability records, regulatory correspondence) must be retained for a minimum of 15 years after study completion or as required by applicable regulation.

\subsection{Monitoring}

Monitoring will be performed by Sponsor-appointed CRAs or a CRO. Site initiation, routine (risk-based, minimum Q3M), and close-out visits are planned. Remote monitoring may be used as appropriate.
