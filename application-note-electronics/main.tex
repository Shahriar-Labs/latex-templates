\section{Scope}
This application note describes a complete reference design for a 5\,V-to-3.3\,V,
2\,A synchronous buck converter intended to power sensor and communication
subsystems in industrial Internet-of-Things (IoT) nodes. It covers the circuit
design, component selection with trade-off rationale, printed-circuit-board
(PCB) layout guidance, and bench validation results for a design built around
the Solstice Semiconductor SSC61830 synchronous buck converter controller.

This document does \emph{not} cover firmware, digital communication interfaces,
or enclosure/mechanical design. Full electrical specifications for the
SSC61830 are provided in the associated datasheet, Reference~\ref{ref:datasheet}.

\section{System Overview and Design Requirements}
Industrial IoT sensor nodes are commonly powered from a 5\,V USB or wall-adapter
rail and require a well-regulated 3.3\,V supply for microcontrollers, radios,
and analog front ends. The converter must remain efficient across a wide load
range, tolerate the ambient temperature extremes of an outdoor enclosure, and
keep switching-noise coupling to a minimum so nearby analog sensor channels are
not disturbed.

\subsection{Functional Requirements}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{REQ-\arabic*:},leftmargin=2.4cm,itemsep=4pt,topsep=4pt]
\item The regulator shall accept an input voltage range of 4.5\,V to 5.5\,V.
\item The regulator shall deliver a regulated output of 3.3\,V $\pm$2\% at up
      to 2\,A continuous load current.
\item Efficiency shall exceed 90\% at 1\,A load, 5\,V input.
\item Output voltage ripple shall not exceed 33\,mV peak-to-peak (1\% of
      $V_{OUT}$) at full load.
\item The design shall operate over an ambient temperature range of
      $-40^\circ$C to $+85^\circ$C.
\item Quiescent supply current in light-load operation shall not exceed
      25\,\textmu A.
\item Conducted EMI emissions shall comply with CISPR~32 Class~B
      limits, Reference~\ref{ref:cispr}.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Target Applications}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.3em,itemsep=2pt]
\item Battery-backed industrial IoT sensor nodes
\item Distributed PLC digital and analog I/O modules
\item Low-power edge gateways and wireless sensor hubs
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Feedback Network Design}
The SSC61830 regulates its feedback (FB) pin to an internal reference,
$V_{REF} = 0.6$\,V. The output voltage is set by the resistive divider $R_1$,
$R_2$ according to:
\begin{equation}
V_{OUT} = V_{REF}\left(1+\dfrac{R_1}{R_2}\right)
\label{eq:fb}
\end{equation}
Choosing $R_2 = 10.0\,\mathrm{k}\Omega$ (a divider current of approximately
60\,\textmu A) and solving Equation~\eqref{eq:fb} for a 3.3\,V output gives
$R_1 = 45.0\,\mathrm{k}\Omega$. Both resistors use 1\% tolerance thin-film
parts to hold the output within the $\pm2\%$ requirement of REQ-2 across
process and temperature.

\section{PCB Layout Recommendations}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.3em,itemsep=3pt]
\item Minimize the area of the high-frequency loop formed by $C_{IN}$, U1, and
      the power ground return; place $C_{IN}$ as close as possible to the
      VIN and GND pins of U1.
\item Route the SW node as a short, wide trace directly to $L_1$; avoid
      routing SW beneath sensitive analog circuitry.
\item Place $R_1$ and $R_2$ close to the FB pin and route the FB trace away
      from SW and $L_1$ to avoid noise coupling into the feedback loop.
\item Use a solid ground plane on an inner layer with multiple vias
      stitching U1's thermal pad to the plane for heat spreading.
\item Keep $C_{OUT}$ close to the load connector to minimize output
      trace inductance and improve transient response.
\item Provide a dedicated thermal relief and generous copper pour on the
      VIN and SW planes to aid conducted heat dissipation.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=cautionbg,colframe=caution,boxrule=0.7pt,arc=1pt,
  left=8pt,right=8pt,top=5pt,bottom=5pt]
\textbf{\color{caution}\faExclamationTriangle\ CAUTION:} Excessive loop area
in the $C_{IN}$--U1--ground path is the most common root cause of switch-node
ringing and CISPR~32 conducted-emission failures observed in early
prototypes. Verify loop area against Figure~\ref{fig:schematic} before
release to production.
\end{tcolorbox}

\section{Reference Design Checklist}
\begin{itemize}[label=$\square$,leftmargin=1.3em,itemsep=5pt]
\item Confirm $V_{IN}$ is present and within 4.5\,V--5.5\,V at the input
      connector before enabling the board.
\item Verify the EN pin transitions high and $V_{OUT}$ rises to
      3.3\,V $\pm2$\% within 5\,ms of enable.
\item Measure output ripple with an AC-coupled scope probe; confirm
      $<$33\,mV peak-to-peak at full load.
\item Record efficiency at 0.2\,A, 1\,A, and 2\,A load points; confirm
      $>$90\% at 1\,A.
\item Perform a 0\,A-to-2\,A load transient test; confirm $V_{OUT}$
      recovers within 2\% in under 50\,\textmu s.
\item Capture a thermal image at 2\,A, 25$^\circ$C ambient, after 10 minutes;
      confirm the U1 case temperature is below 105$^\circ$C.
\item Verify the FB trace is routed away from the SW node and $L_1$ per
      Section~8.
\item Confirm the silkscreen revision marking matches the assembly drawing
      revision (Rev.~C).
\item Run a conducted-emissions pre-scan and compare against the
      CISPR~32 Class~B limit.
\item Archive all test data and sign off in the engineering change order
      (ECO) system.
\end{itemize}
