\section{Executive Summary}

Brightleaf Coffee Co. has grown from a single roastery to six specialty cafe locations across
Central Texas, yet its digital presence has not kept pace with the business. The current website
was built in 2019, converts poorly on mobile, and offers no way for customers to manage a coffee
subscription online -- a gap that is quietly pushing recurring revenue toward third-party
marketplaces that keep the customer relationship and the margin.

Horizon Digital Studio proposes a focused ten-week engagement to rebuild Brightleaf's website on
a modern, mobile-first foundation, launch a native subscription-commerce experience, and pair the
new site with a coordinated SEO and paid-social campaign for the relaunch. Our objective is
straightforward: turn Brightleaf's website into its highest-performing sales channel.

\begin{tcolorbox}[noteboxstyle]
  \textbf{\color{accent}What success looks like:} a 35\% increase in online subscription
  sign-ups within two quarters of launch, a mobile checkout completion rate above 68\%, and
  a website Brightleaf's team can update themselves without calling a developer.
\end{tcolorbox}

\section{Objectives}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Rebuild the Brightleaf website on a fast, mobile-first platform with a design system
        that reflects the brand's craft-roastery positioning.
  \item Launch native subscription commerce so customers can start, pause, or modify a coffee
        subscription without leaving brightleafcoffee.com.
  \item Improve organic search visibility for high-intent local and category terms ahead of the
        fall wholesale season.
  \item Equip the Brightleaf marketing team with a content editor they can use independently
        post-launch.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Scope of Work}

\subsection{Discovery \& Strategy}
\begin{checklist}
  \item Stakeholder interviews with Brightleaf leadership and cafe managers
  \item Competitive and category audit (8 comparable specialty roasters)
  \item Customer journey mapping and sitemap for the new site
  \item Technical SEO baseline audit of the existing site
\end{checklist}

\subsection{Design \& Development}
\begin{checklist}
  \item Custom visual design system: typography, color, photography direction
  \item 12 responsive page templates (home, shop, subscription, locations, journal, etc.)
  \item Headless CMS build (content editable without developer support)
  \item Accessibility pass targeting WCAG 2.1 AA
\end{checklist}

\subsection{E-Commerce \& Integrations}
\begin{checklist}
  \item Recurring subscription billing with three tiers (Weekly, Biweekly, Monthly)
  \item Shipping-rate rules and local-pickup option for all six cafe locations
  \item Integration with existing POS and inventory system
  \item Order and subscription management dashboard for the Brightleaf team
\end{checklist}

\subsection{Marketing \& Launch}
\begin{checklist}
  \item On-page SEO implementation across all migrated content (20 pages)
  \item Six-week paid social and email campaign timed to launch
  \item Analytics and conversion tracking setup (GA4 and Synapse Pixel)
  \item Staff walkthrough and CMS training session
\end{checklist}

\section{Project Timeline}

The engagement runs ten weeks from kickoff to launch, structured in five phases so Brightleaf's
team always knows what to expect and when their input is needed.

\begin{timeline}
  \item \textbf{\color{accent}Discovery \& Strategy} \hfill {\color{accent2}\textbf{Weeks 1--2}}\\[2pt]
        \small\color{softgray} Stakeholder interviews, competitive audit, sitemap, and technical
        SEO baseline. Ends with a strategy sign-off meeting.

  \item \textbf{\color{accent}UX \& Visual Design} \hfill {\color{accent2}\textbf{Weeks 3--4}}\\[2pt]
        \small\color{softgray} Wireframes, visual design system, and 12 responsive page templates
        delivered for review and approval.

  \item \textbf{\color{accent}Development \& Integration} \hfill {\color{accent2}\textbf{Weeks 5--7}}\\[2pt]
        \small\color{softgray} Headless CMS build, subscription commerce setup, POS integration,
        and analytics instrumentation.

  \item \textbf{\color{accent}Testing \& QA} \hfill {\color{accent2}\textbf{Week 8}}\\[2pt]
        \small\color{softgray} Cross-device QA, accessibility pass, checkout and subscription flow
        testing, staff training session.

  \item \textbf{\color{accent}Launch \& Marketing Rollout} \hfill {\color{accent2}\textbf{Weeks 9--10}}\\[2pt]
        \small\color{softgray} Production launch, six-week paid social and email campaign begins,
        post-launch monitoring.
\end{timeline}

\vspace{0.8em}
\begin{tcolorbox}[noteboxstyle]
  \textbf{\color{accent}Dependency note:} Phase durations assume feedback and asset approvals
  from Brightleaf within two business days of each review milestone. Longer review cycles shift
  the launch date accordingly.
\end{tcolorbox}
