\section{Overview \& Goals}

The \textbf{Event Stream Processor (ESP)} is a horizontally scalable microservice
responsible for ingesting, filtering, transforming, and routing high-throughput
event streams from internal producers (mobile clients, IoT sensors, backend
services) to downstream consumers (analytics warehouse, alerting engine,
third-party webhooks).

\textbf{Goals:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em, itemsep=2pt, topsep=3pt]
  \item Handle $\geq 500{,}000$ events/sec sustained with ${<}\,10\,\mathrm{ms}$ p99 latency.
  \item Guarantee at-least-once delivery with idempotent consumer support.
  \item Provide a declarative routing DSL for schema-validated event pipelines.
  \item Achieve zero-downtime rolling deploys and automated canary promotion.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Non-goals:} Stateful aggregations (owned by Analytics Service);
long-term event storage (owned by the Data Lake).

The \textbf{Ingestion Gateway} terminates TLS, authenticates producers (API key /
service account), and forwards validated raw events to the \textbf{ESP Core
Processor} over an internal gRPC stream. The Core Processor evaluates declarative
routing rules, applies transformation plugins, and publishes to \textbf{Kafka}. The
\textbf{Schema Registry} (Confluent-compatible) is consulted on every event type;
unknown schemas are rejected with \texttt{422 Unprocessable Entity}.

\section{Detailed Design}

\subsection{Routing DSL}

Pipelines are declared in YAML and compiled to a binary filter tree at startup:

\begin{tcolorbox}[
  enhanced, sharp corners,
  colback=gray!5, colframe=rule, boxrule=0.5pt,
  fontupper=\small\ttfamily,
  left=6pt, right=6pt, top=4pt, bottom=4pt,
  before skip=4pt, after skip=4pt
]
pipeline: iot-telemetry-v2\\
\phantom{xx}filters:\\
\phantom{xxxx}- field: event\_type\\
\phantom{xxxxxxxx}eq: sensor.reading\\
\phantom{xxxx}transforms: [mask\_pii, add\_region]\\
\phantom{xxxx}route\_to: kafka://telemetry.raw
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection{Transformation Plugin API}

Plugins implement the \texttt{Transform} interface (Go):

\begin{tcolorbox}[
  enhanced, sharp corners,
  colback=gray!5, colframe=rule, boxrule=0.5pt,
  fontupper=\small\ttfamily,
  left=6pt, right=6pt, top=4pt, bottom=4pt,
  before skip=4pt, after skip=4pt
]
type Transform interface \{\\
\phantom{xxxx}Name() string\\
\phantom{xxxx}Apply(ctx context.Context, e *Event) (*Event, error)\\
\}
\end{tcolorbox}

Plugins are loaded as Go plugins at startup; hot-reload requires a rolling
restart. Execution order follows declaration order in the pipeline YAML.
A plugin returning an error triggers the dead-letter-queue (DLQ) path.

\subsection{State \& Persistence}

ESP Core is intentionally stateless. Offset management is delegated to Kafka
consumer groups. Pipeline configuration is stored in etcd with watch-based
live propagation ($<\!500\,\mathrm{ms}$ propagation SLO).

\section{Testing \& Validation}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em, itemsep=3pt, topsep=3pt]
  \item \textbf{Unit tests (Go):} $\geq 85\%$ line coverage enforced in CI; table-driven
        tests for all filter/transform combinations.
  \item \textbf{Integration tests:} Docker Compose environment with Kafka, etcd, and a
        mock Schema Registry; contract-tested via Pact.
  \item \textbf{Load test:} \texttt{k6} scenario at 600\,k\,ev/s for 10\,min;
        p99 latency assertion ${<}\,10\,\mathrm{ms}$; automated via Argo Workflows post-deploy.
  \item \textbf{Chaos:} Gremlin fault injection \textendash{} pod kill, 200\,ms network delay,
        Kafka broker eviction \textendash{} verifying at-least-once delivery is maintained.
  \item \textbf{Security scan:} \texttt{govulncheck} + Trivy image scan in every PR;
        high/critical CVEs block merge.
\end{itemize}

\section{Risks}

\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}llllX@{}}
  \toprule
  \textbf{ID} & \textbf{Risk} & \textbf{Likelihood} & \textbf{Impact} & \textbf{Mitigation} \\
  \midrule
  R-01 & Plugin ABI instability   & Medium & High     &
        Pin Go plugin build tags; integration-test every plugin in CI. \\[3pt]
  R-02 & etcd watch storm         & Low    & Medium   &
        Jitter propagation; circuit-breaker on watch re-subscribe. \\[3pt]
  R-03 & Kafka lag spike          & Medium & High     &
        Auto-scale consumers; DLQ with exponential backoff replay. \\[3pt]
  R-04 & Schema Registry outage   & Low    & High     &
        Local LRU cache (5\,min TTL); fail-open configurable per pipeline. \\[3pt]
  R-05 & PII leakage in traces    & Low    & Critical &
        Trace data redacted by \texttt{mask\_pii} before storage; field-level ACLs. \\
  \bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
